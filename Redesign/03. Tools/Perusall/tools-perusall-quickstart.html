Perusall enables students to collaboratively annotate documents of your choice. Students can start a new annotation thread in Perusall by simply selecting a piece of text or an image and typing a question or comment, which other students can reply to or upvote. Perusall's intended use is to stimulate students to complete the required reading before lectures, so that you can continue discussions or address questions from the annotations during the lecture itself.

Quickstart

This quickstart covers all the steps you need to complete to use the tool. If you skip any of the steps below, the tool might not function properly. For more information on specific topics, please check the menu on the right.

1. Create a Perusall assignment in Canvas

  1. Go to Assignments in the left menu of your Canvas course
  2. Click on + Assignment
  3. Fill in 10 as the maximum number of points
  4. Select External tool as the submission type.
  5. Choose Perusall 1.3 from the list of external tools
  6. Click Link to home page in the screen that pops up
  7. Click on Select
  8. Tick the box in front of Load this tool in a new tab
  9. Name the assignment, assign it to everyone, fill in a due date and click Save

2. Add documents to Perusall

  1. Go to the Perusall assignment you've created in step 1
  2. Click on Load [assignment name here] in a new window to open Perusall
  3. Go to the Library tab
  4. Select + Add content
  5. Click on Documents from my device to upload a document from your own computer
  6. Select the document you wish to upload to Perusall

3. Link your document(s) to an assignment

You've added your document to Perusall. The next step is to create an assignment to which the document is attached.

  1. Go to the Assignments tab
  2. Click on Add assignment twice
  3. Select the document you wish to link to this assignment by unfolding the menu next to Content to assign
  4. Choose to assign all content or to only assign page ranges of the document
  5. Add multiple documents (if you wish) by clicking + Add another part in the top left corner
  6. Click Next step at the bottom right corner to go to the Options tab
  7. Customize the options to your liking. In any case, you are required to fill in a submission deadline.
  8. Click OK to create the assignment

    We'll adjust the scoring settings at step 4 of this quickstart, so at this point there's no need to adjust anything in the scoring tab.

4. Establish groups within Perusall

If all students were to annotate one and the same document in Perusall, it'd quickly turn unreadable as a result of all the highlighted pieces of text from the annotations. To prevent this, Perusall has a grouping feature which distributes students across groups. Students can only see the annotations of their fellow group members.

  1. Click on the Settings menu in Perusall.
  2. Go to Grouping
  3. You can choose to either let Perusall distribute students randomly or specify groups yourself.

    Distribute students randomly: Simply fill in the number of students in your course and the desired group size. Please note, Perusall will create new group distributions for every single document when using the automatic group distribution feature. If you want students to stay in the same group throughout the course, you must specify the groups yourself.

    Specify groups: Simply fill in a group number in front of the student name to assign a student to that group or copy over the groups from Canvas.

  4. Click Save changes to save the group settings

5. Configure scoring settings

Before configuring the grade calculation, you'll need to configure a few scoring settings which influence how and when grades are sent to Canvas.

  1. Click on the Settings menu in Perusall
  2. Go to General below Scoring
  3. Set Release score to students to either:

    After instructor manually releases: if you want to press a button in the Perusall gradebook to release the grades.

    After the assignment deadline has passed: if you want the grades to be automatically released in Perusall once the assignment deadline has passed.

    Releasing the scores immediately after submitting is discouraged, as this incentivizes students to game Perusall's scoring before the deadline.

  4. Set the lowest possible score to 0
  5. Make sure the maximum score is equal to the number of points the assignment is worth in Canvas. If these numbers do not match, the grades in Perusall and Canvas will differ.
  6. Go to LMS grade sync below Scoring
  7. Set Grade sync to LMS to either:

    Manually sync assignment scores to LMS: if you want to press a button in the Perusall gradebook to send the grades to Canvas.

    Automatically sync assignment score to LMS: if you want the grades to be automatically sent to Canvas once the assignment deadline has passed. Please note, it can take up to two hours before the grades are automatically sent to Canvas.

6. Set up automatic grading

Now that the general scoring settings have been configured, you can start configuring the automatic grading.

  1. Go to Automatic scoring below Scoring
  2. Select a scoring preset. Hollistic is generally a good starting point.
  3. Tweak the scoring preset. We suggest the following:
    • Check whether the options of all scoring criteria fit your texts. For example, if your texts are very short, you might want to lower the active engagement time and the number of required comments.
    • Have the scoring weights exceed 100% by about 10 to 40 percent. This way students have various ways to earn full credits.
    • Modify the number of high quality comments under the annotation content options to a number of comments that fits the length, complexity and discussability of your text.
    • Have annotation content, getting responses and upvoting make up the largest bulk of your scoring.
    • Make sure it's possible to attain a (near) 100% score without active reading time, reading to the end and opening the assignment. Some students might prefer to read on paper rather than from Perusall
    • Have the scoring weights exceed 100% by about 10 to 40 percent so that students have various ways to earn full credits.

      You can also modify your scoring per assignment in the assignment settings instead, in case your texts vary greatly.

7. Select the correct annotation language

Perusall can automatically score both Dutch and English annotation content, so it's important to select the correct language before your assignments start.

  1. Click on the Settings menu in Perusall
  2. Go to General
  3. Select your desired Comment language
  4. Click Save changes at the bottom of the page

8. Inform your students

Since many students might not have used a application like Perusall before, it's important to properly inform them about how Perusall works and what is expected of them. We recommend the following:

  • Communicate the number of high quality comments needed to receive the maximum score on annotation content.
  • Do not inform students of the other criteria thresholds for a maximum score. You don't have to specify exactly how many minutes students should spend reading a document, as this might invite students to try to game the system. It's also beyond the purpose of Perusall, i.e. starting discussions and asking questions.
  • Define what a high quality comment is. You can share this page with students, which explains how to write high quality comments.
  • Advise students to annotate well before the deadline, as they are unlikely to receive responses and/or upvotes when placing comments a few hours before the deadline.
  • Instruct students to flag annotatoins in cases of plagiarism or inappropriate content. You will receive an e-mail with the flagged comment, so you can assess it.
  • Inform students that they can view how their annotations were scored when the deadline has passed under 'My scores' in Perusall.
  • Communicate to students that you will be reading the comments to use them for your lectures and to manually adjust scores (if applicable).
  • Inform students that they have to open Perusall via Canvas at least once. If they don't, their grade might not propagate to the Canvas gradebook, in which case you'll have to do this manually.
