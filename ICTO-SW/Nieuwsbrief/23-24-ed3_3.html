New copyright regulations: check your teaching materials!

When using or reusing copyrighted material in education, lecturers have to deal with laws and regulations surrounding copyright. For the 2023-2025 period, new rules apply with regard to copyright. Though the new agreement has only just been made public, the arrangements are effective from this year onwards. You are, for example, allowed to copy up to 40 pages (instead of 50) from a publication, provided this constitutes a maximum of 20% of that work. With images and audiovisual material, you must always cite the source. This also applies to, for instance, (PowerPoint) presentations, knowledge clips and web lectures. By using this copyright checker, you can be sure you are on the right track!

   NEW! The UvA switched from Mediasite to Panopto

From Mediasite to Panopto
After the Christmas holidays, the web lecture service made a transition to a new supplier, resulting in the replacement of 35 recorders in the lecture halls. Starting from January 8th, lectures will no longer be recorded using Mediasite; instead, they will be recorded using the new system Panopto. The procedure for requesting web lectures will stay unchanged, and utilizing the new system is fairly straightforward.

Accessing old videos
In the meantime, new content can be accessed through Panopto, while old videos can still be viewed on Mediasite, and they will remain accessible until the migration is fully completed.

Technical issues
With the switch to Panopto, you may have encountered some initial challenges. Technical issues arose during the first week, but they have since been resolved. Unfortunately, there are also some limitations with Panopto, as it is currently not available through the rich content editor in Canvas, unlike Mediasite. This means that teachers can't easily access lectures and embed them as they could before. The web lecture service is working to address this issue, but it is uncertain when this option will be reinstated in the future.
Moreover, in the lecture halls, the message "recording" is currently not displayed on the touch panel as soon as a recording starts. Upon successful recording, the message "not recording" remains on screen. We are working hard to find a solution. Recording lectures still works the same as with Mediasite. Always contact the web lecture service when in doubt about a started recording (020-5252800).

   Establish which basic rules you must follow for linking sources

For textual sources, the following conditions apply:
→ Maximum of 40 pages from a publication;
→ No more than 20% of the original publication.

For example:
Does a publication count 100 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of 20 pages from this. Does a publication count 20 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of four pages.

Please note that 'original publication' means the number / issue / edition of the journal in which the article was published.

→ Here, you can read on what to do if you do not meet these conditions

   Brush up your knowledge on which things are always allowed

In terms of linking sources, there are many cases where the above mentioned conditions do not apply. Below it is described which cases are always allowed, but you can also always use the Copyright Checker:

→ Linking to materials in the Library Catalogue CataloguePlus;
→ Linking to Open Access publications (with or without a Creative Commons licence);
→ Linking to any other place where a publication can legally be accessed;
→ Uploading material from databases for which the Library has a licence, if the database allows this.
→ Uploading non-commercial educational material created by yourself or at the UvA;
→ Referring to materials and having students look up the physical publication themselves;
→ Using or reusing publications that are in the public domain;
→ Linking to or embedding audiovisual material. You may show audiovisual material to aid in teaching on-site. Uploading audiovisual material in a digital learning environment is not allowed.

Go to Modules in your course menu and create a new module by clicking on the upper right button +Module. Now, add the study guide to this module. You can do so by clicking on the upper right button + within the module. Then you can select the guide as a file from your local computer.

   Organize your Canvas course

Use the following tips to organize your Canvas course and keep it neat and tidy. This not only benefits your students, but also yourself and your colleagues.

1) Use the course menu Modules as your course home page
You can make use of many options and features in Canvas, such as a Pages, an Assignment or a File. For each one of these, there's a different menu item. However, there is one exception: Modules. If you're using modules, you can actually import and combine all these elements together to create a clear overview. Instead of students having to click through different kinds of course menus and items, you can now provide a structured setup.

→ How do I use modules in my Canvas course?
→ How do I change the Course Home Page?

2) Minimize your course menu
For each course, there are at least 20 different kinds of menus to make use of, you are probably not going to use all of these. We strongly recommend that you hide any parts you're not using, otherwise students aren't able to properly navigate the course content.
