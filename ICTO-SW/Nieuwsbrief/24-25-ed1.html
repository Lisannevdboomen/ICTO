Digital examination software: Ans

As you have probably already heard, the UvA will transition to a new software system for digital testing in September 2024: Ans!

Will you be administering digital exams this semester? Sign up for the Ans training sessions offered by the ICTO teams of the Faculty of Society & Behaviour. Two training sessions will be given at various moments: one training session is about creating an exam and the other about reviewing an exam.

In addition to the general training sessions, walk-ins are also organized. As a teacher you can just walk in with your laptop so we can answer your questions on the spot. Click the button below for the available dates.
Log in on Ans Trainings & walk-ins

Final Grades update

The grade calculation tool within Canvas has undergone an update this year. The tool works largely the same but looks a little different than you may be used to. One benefit of the update is that the tool now works a lot faster, especially in courses with a large number of students.

Did you have difficulty preparing the calculation set-up last year? Or did you run into some issues submitting the grades to SIS? Perhaps you just have some very complicated ideas on how you’d like to calculate the final grade. Whatever the case, we strongly encourage you to set up a meeting with us by making an appointment in our booking form (by selecting 'Final Grades support').

You can make an appointment via the link below. This can be done online or on location.
Book an appointment

Assigning modules by section

A new functionality within Canvas that we would like to point out to you is the ability to assign modules to certain sections of students.

This works in a similar way that you can assign assign assignments or quizzes to sections, and it allows you to organize your Canvas course even more conveniently. The button below will take you to instructions that explain more about how you can structure your Canvas course.
Using modules to structure your course

Creating practice materials in Ans & Canvas

Did you know that it is possible to create practice tests in Ans, as well as creating Canvas quizzes for students to practice?

This allows you to familiarize students with the way of testing and introduce them to the ANS platform. By offering practice tests, students can better prepare and get used to the examination software, which can improve their performance during the actual exam. Want to get started with this? Contact ICTO for support and more information.

Using feedback tools

Did you know you can easily organize peer reviews in Canvas?

This allows students to give each other feedback on their work, which provides a valuable learning experience. Students develop their critical thinking skills and learn to give and receive feedback constructively, which can significantly improve their learning process. If you are interested in incorporating this into your course, please contact ICTO for more information and support.

Privacy in digital education

All teachers at the university need to handle personal data of students and colleagues with care, in line with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). It is strongly discouraged to store such data locally, on private devices, such as personal laptops or smartphones.

Instead, always use the secure university laptop and solutions within the Office 365 package for storing and processing personal data. When sharing files related to groups, such as student lists or results, it is essential to send as little information as possible. Avoid sharing entire email lists or other sensitive data in emails, espaecially when communicating with students. Use secure platforms and only share strictly necessary information. These practices help protect the privacy of everyone involved and meet the GDPR requirements. For more information on the ICT Code of Conduct, please click the button below.
