New digital examination platform Ans

As you have may already heard, the UvA will transition to a new software system for digital testing in September 2024, Ans! From now on, TestVision will only be available in 'read-only' mode.

Will you be administering digital exams this semester or are you curious about the new software? Sign up for the Ans training sessions offered by the ICTO teams of our faculty. Two training sessions will be given at various moments: one about creating an exam and the other about reviewing exams.

Would you prefer to get started independently? Then we recommend you to complete the e-learning (English or Dutch). You can use your own test course in Ans' test environment by requesting access through this form. In addition to the general training sessions, walk-ins are organized. As a teacher you can just walk in with your laptop so we can answer your questions on the spot.

Final Grades has been upgraded

The grade calculation tool within Canvas has undergone an update this year. The tool works largely the same but looks a little different than you may be used to. One benefit of the update is that the tool now should work faster, especially in courses with a large number of students.

Additionally, the central IT team will be optimizing the interface and technical capabilities of the grade calculation system during the upcoming year. They would highly appreciate your feedback through one-on-one brainstorming sessions and testing in September. If you're interested in participating, please let us know at your earliest convenience.

Did you have difficulty preparing the calculation set-up last year? Or did you run into some issues submitting the grades to SIS? Perhaps you just have some very complicated ideas on how you’d like to calculate the final grade. Whatever the case, we strongly encourage you to set up a meeting with us by making an appointment with us through our booking form (select 'Final Grades support').

Assign modules to certain groups

A new functionality within Canvas that we would like to point out to you is the ability to assign modules to specific sections of students, such as individual students or tutorial groups.

This feature works similarly to how you can currently assign assignments or quizzes to different sections, offering you greater flexibility and convenience in organizing your Canvas course content. This functionality is fully supported on both laptops and mobile devices, ensuring the right accessibility for students. FeedbackFruits will be phased out

As of January 2025, the UvA will no longer offer FeedbackFruits in Canvas. The reason for discontinuing the license is that FeedbackFruits is used very little, and the cost will increase dramatically starting next year.

Luckily, in most cases, there are good alternatives. For example, instead of creating a peer-review in FeedbackFruits, the peer-review function of Canvas itself can be used, or you might want to take a look at the peer review functionality of Ans (our new digital examination system). If these tools are not working for you, please contact ICTO to see if we can help find other alternatives.

Other parts of FeedbackFruits that you may have used – such as an Interactive Document or Evaluation – can maybe also be replaced by other tools. If necessary, the ICTO team will do its best to find a good alternative for the assignments you are currently using FeedbackFruits for.

Creating formative exercises with Ans

Did you know that it is possible to create practice tests in Ans, as well as peer review or self review assignments?

Ans is a powerful tool for creating formative exercises like practice tests, peer reviews, and self-review assignments. It offers a user-friendly environment with more customizable settings than Canvas, allowing teachers to tailor exercises to their course needs. This flexibility enhances student engagement and learning outcomes. Overall, we encourage you to try out Ans as it provides a more efficient and versatile platform and it allows you to familiarize students with the way of testing in the ANS platform. Want to get started? Take a look in https://ans.uva.nl/ yourself, view our information page and contact ICTO if you need support.

Embedding web lectures

A new tool will soon be available in Canvas that will let you embed web lectures directly into your course pages. By embedding lectures this way, students won't be able to share direct links with others.

The web lecture service is diligently working to make this tool available, but they've encountered unexpected issues that delayed the planned launch for Semester 1. However, it will be up soon, and ICTO will notify teachers when it's ready. Meanwhile, please share lecture links directly with students. If you'd like all lectures embedded on a Canvas page as a playlist, email ICTO for the embed code. This allows you to set dates and assign students, but note that the URL might still be discoverable.

All lectures from the old system Mediasite (through January 2024) have been migrated to the new system Panopto. Lectures from the past two years are organized within the Panopto folder structure. If you need assistance accessing lectures, please contact the web lecture service (webcolleges@uva.nl).

You can continue to request web lectures as usual through the webcollegeplanner. If you have any questions, feel free to reach out to the web lecture service.

Copyright rules in education

For the 2023-2025 period, new rules apply with regard to copyright. Check that your teaching materials are still compliant: it is important that rules regarding copyright for educational material are observed because infringement can lead to fines for the UvA.

Lecturers are now allowed to copy up to 40 pages (instead of 50) from a publication, provided this constitutes a maximum of 20% of that work. The new rules come from an amendment to the Easy Access Agreement (formerly the 'reader regulation') that has been agreed between Dutch universities (UNL) and publishers (UvO Foundation). For more information check the article on the UvA web. Please note that this restriction also applies for documents used in the tool Perusall.

Privacy in digital education

All teachers at the university need to handle personal data of students and colleagues with care, in line with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). It is strongly discouraged to store such data locally, on private devices, such as personal laptops or smartphones.

Instead, always use the secure university laptop and solutions within the Office 365 package for storing and processing personal data. When sharing files related to groups, such as student lists or results, it is essential to send as little information as possible. Avoid sharing entire email lists or other sensitive data in emails, espaecially when communicating with students. Use secure platforms and only share strictly necessary information. These practices help protect the privacy of everyone involved and meet the GDPR requirements. For more information on the ICT Code of Conduct, please click the button below.
