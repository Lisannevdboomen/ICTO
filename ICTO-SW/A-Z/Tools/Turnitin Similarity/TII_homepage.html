At the UvA, the plagiarism detection software Turnitin Similarity is available to check submitted assignments for similarity.

After students submit their assignments, Turnitin scans the content for similarities with other sources. If the assignment has a set deadline, Turnitin will repeat the scan after the deadline to also include the other students' content in the similarity check.


'Similarity' vs. 'plagiarism'
Turnitinâ€™s software takes the content of a student's submission and compares it to a massive database of content, including internet, academic, and student paper content, and looks for similarities. It reports those similarities with a percentage, the percentage of the work submitted by the student that is similar to the content in the databases. In the report, a teacher can dig into the details to see what, exactly, is matching, and how much.
