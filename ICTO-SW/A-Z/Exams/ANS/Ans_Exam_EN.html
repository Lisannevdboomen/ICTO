In the academic year 2023-2024 the UvA will transition to a new software system for digital exams. This is the result of a statuatory European tender. Two suppliers had registered for this tender. After an extensive evaluation, Ans Exam emerged as the best choice. The current supplier, TestVision, came in second. This means that all faculties of the UvA will gradually switch to Ans Exam during the upcoming academic year.

With Ans Exam, the UvA brings in an innovative partner for the long term, with a contract of at least 6 years, extendable up to a maximum of 15 years. This long duration forms the basis for stable service and collaboration. ANS Exam has extensive experience in higher education with digital examinations. The continuous development and innovation of Ans Exam will also be shaped based on the specific wishes of the UvA.

The current examination system, TestVision, will remain available at least until August 2024, so that teachers and administrators can continue to use it during the transition phase to Ans Exam. Click here to read the frequently asked questions about the transition from TestVision to Ans.

Ans Exam offers fresh and new functionalities. We are very pleased with its intuitive design and believe that teachers will be better able to handle functionalities related to constructing or reviewing an exam. Here is a summary:

   New & friendly design

Ans is an innovative system and has a modern appearance. Unlike TestVision, you can now create the exam in a single environment (meaning that all question and exam settings are found within the same menu). Do you want to save or reuse questions for, for example, formative assessments? Make sure to use the intelligent item bank. This webpage describes all the available settings.


   Annotate text while grading

We hear that teachers are very pleased with the grading functionalities of Ans. You can now conveniently annotate sections of text or use quick comments. As a result, grading has become much easier and more akin to assessing with Canvas SpeedGrader.


   Flag submissions for feedback

Are you uncertain about a grading and would like to enlist the help of colleagues? You can now 'flag' a question, thereby asking your colleagues for feedback. The other reviewer(s) receive a notification in Ans and can review with you. This entire process is hidden from students. View this webpage for more explanation about flagging questions.


   Easily adjust scores and grading schemes retroactively

Ans Exam understands that it's only after grading an exam that the true nature of the grading scheme becomes clear. It's now easier for teachers to adjust the scores or the grading scheme. View this webpage for more explanation on how to deviate from the grading scheme.


   Add graders yourself and distribute questions

Finally! No more emailing ICTO and waiting for graders to be added, because in Ans you can easily do this yourself. Additionally, it has become much simpler to distribute the grading among colleagues or, for example, to distribute it among tutorial groups. View this webpage for more information about the distribution of grading.


   Use the smart question bank

The simplest method in Ans is to create questions directly from a course exam. But do you want to save questions? Or reuse them? Then save the questions to the item bank of the course, and link tags or learning objectives to questions so that you and your colleagues build a smart database. Filter through the question database to quickly prepare formative or summative item bank exams and import them into the course exam. Read more about question banks here.


   Fewer error-prone settings

All settings in Ans that pose a risk to the exam administration are displayed within a clearly recognizable 'danger zone'. The system places the settings within a red outline with a warning next to it, ensuring you know which settings are safe to experiment with and which are not.


   Automated exam settings

We are continuously taking steps to automate processes. In the past year, significant changes have occurred in the logistics behind our systems. With the introduction of Ans, it is now possible to automatically integrate courses and exams from the source systems into Ans. In addition, course coordinators can be directly linked to exams, and exam details such as time, location, participants, and extra time are automatically filled in. This significantly contributes to reducing errors during administration. We are pleased with these developments and hope that this considerably improves the experience for both students and teachers.
