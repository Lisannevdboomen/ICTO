Connection between final grades and TestVision

It has become a lot easier to export student results from TestVision to Canvas! From now on, grades are directly visible in final grades, so no more hassle of importing and exporting Excel files. Previously, the grade calculation in TestVision allowed you to see grades obtained by students. Because of the direct link between TestVision and Canvas, this calculation is no longer done in TestVision, but in Final Grades itself. This means youâ€™ll only be able to see the percentages students have achieved. These percentages are a direct reflection of the points obtained compared to the total number of points. The grade calculation in Final Grades is linear and a chance score for multiple-choice questions is included.

How do I validate the results?
How do I see these results?

1. Go to Sessions in TestVision and select the relevant one

2. Click on Results at the right of the relevant session

 

How to retrieve grades
1. Go to the Final Grades menu and open the grade Calculation Setup
 

2. Click on the top right button Add partial result and select Existing assignment
 


3. A new window will pop-up. Go to TestVision Online, select the exam and press OK
 
Can't find the exam? Please email icto-csw@uva.nl 
4. The exam is added to the Grade Calculation. Assign a weight to the exam in the concerning box.
 

 
6. Go back to the grade list, navigate to Grade Calculation and select Retrieve TestVision grades
 

7. Now click on Recalculate grades in the upper right and select Recalculate all grades
 

 

 

How to adjust caesura
1. Go to the Final Grades menu and open the grade Calculation Setup
 

2. Click on the exam
 

3. Scroll down and click on More options
 

4. Go to Grade type and switch to Grades (1-10)
 

 

Students who took the test separately

In some cases, students have been allowed to take the test separately at Roeterseiland (special facilities). If this is the case with your course, please let us know by emailing to icto-csw@uva.nl. We will add an extra item in the calculation in which you can put the grade of the student(s).
