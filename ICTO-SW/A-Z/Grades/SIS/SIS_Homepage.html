Send grades to SIS

How to submit both final grades (1st try) and retake grades (2nd try) to SIS

    Submit Grades to SIS

   SIS Grade Correction

Use the correction-mode when:

You've already submitted grades to SIS, but you need to adjust one or more grades. For example, after the exam inspection has taken place. If this is the case, a correction must be made in order to submit the correct grades to SIS.

Grades have been submitted to SIS when you see one (1st try) or two (2nd try) green checkmarks in front of the students' names in the Final Grades tool.

    When to use corrections?

Contact

Who to contact when something goes wrong or you have questions

If you don't see a red Submit grades button, please get in touch with your Programme Coordinator or Manager. They can give you the 'Examiner' rights in SIS. Please note that only the coördinator of the course can submit grades to SIS. 

If you have successfully submitted your grades to SIS, but there is an issue of some sort or if you have a questions about your grades in SIS, please get in touch with the Registrar's office (gradeadmin-sw@uva.nl).

 

Symbols in gradelist

After you've submitted grades to SIS, the gradelist will display symbols in front of students' names. You do not need to act on these symbols, the administration office will contact you when you do.

 

 = The final grade is currently being transferred to SIS.

= The final grade has been successfully transferred to SIS.

 = The resit grade has been successfully transferred to SIS.

= You can no longer submit grades to SIS for this student because (a) they've successfully completed the course, or (b) both the final grade and resit grade have been submitted to SIS.

 =  You cannot transfer grades for this student

 =  This student is not enrolled in SIS for this course.

=  This grade was imported from a file or entered or modified manually.

= Unfortunately, an error occurred during the transfer. You don't have to act on this error! The administration office will automatically receive a message and they will contact you if anything seems wrong. In most cases, this icon only indicates errors that are excepted.
