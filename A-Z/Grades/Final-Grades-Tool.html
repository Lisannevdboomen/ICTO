About the Final Grades Tool

The Final Grades Tool offers lecturers a faster and easier way to submit grades from Canvas to SIS.

There are three ways to get your grades in this tool:

  • Import grades from Canvas

  • Import grades using an Excel workbook

  • Enter the final grades manually

 

This tool allows you to calculate Final grades from different (Canvas) elements. Setup your grade calculation using Canvas Assignments, Quizzes, Grades, Excel Imports, et cetera. Then calculate your students' final grades using the possibilities of the Final grades tool's options and features. Test the calculation setup before publishing it to students. After review, you can submit the final grades to SIS using Two-Factor Authentication.

How to access

  Final Grades menu   Grade calculation setup

 

Examples

Below you will find three different examples (i.e. types) of grade calculations. These examples can be viewed in video form or as instructions with images.

Basic
  • 4 Canvas assignments that are each worth a maximum of 10 points.
  • These assignments need to have their own mutual weight of 3 x 20% and 1 x 40%
  View basic example
Intermediate
  • Students only have to make 3 out of 4 assignments
  • These assignments will count for 35% towards the final grade
  • The other parts of this course need to weigh 50% and 15%
  View intermediate example
Advanced
  • Canvas Exams (i.e. Canvas Quizzes) is included in this setup
  • These quizzes are worth 15 points in total so they need to be scaled to a grade of 10 points
  • The lowest grade will be dropped
  • The quizzes are in two versions: one English version and one Dutch
  View advanced example

Submit grades to the SiS-Administration

Step 0

Check the grades

 

If you haven't done so already, double check if all the grades are correct (especially students who scored around the 5,5 mark) 

Step 1

Select for whom you want to submit grades, at Grade list for:      

 

If you are the examiner of the whole course, select All students

If you are the examiner of a certain section, select Section

If you would like to select certain students, then select the red boxes at the left

 
Step 2 Click on the Submit grades button at the right
   
Step 3 Make sure that Final grade is selected from the dropdown box at the top.
 

Your screen shows all the final grades of the students.

 

 

 
Step 4

Press Submit
 


 
Step 4 A pop-up window will open where you can start the 2FA process
 

Please make sure your browser enables pop-ups. 
Step 5

Click on Sign grade list using two-factor authentication
 

   
Step 6 Two-factor authentication
 

Now follow the steps that will be offered to you. First of all you will probably be asked to connect to the relevant Surf service using your UvAnetID

Then you can two-factor authenticate, using tiqr, sms or ubikey (however you have set this up in the past). In this example we use the tiqr app.

Simply follow the steps that are provided, even though they may differ slightly from the screenshots below.

Step 7

Confirmation and optional download of grade list
 

You will receive a message that This grade list has been signed and confirmed.

 

If so desired, Download a PDF grade list.
Step 8

Return to the Final grades menu and reload
 

 

You will now see a green tick in front of the students' names that have successfully been submitted to SIS!

 

Legenda symbolen:

 =  You cannot transfer grades for this student

 =  This student is not enrolled in SIS for this course.

=  This grade was imported from a file or entered or modified manually.

= This grade has been transferred to SIS but has not yet arrived in SIS.

= The final grade has been successfully transferred to SIS.

 = The resit grade has been successfully transferred to SIS.

= Unfortunately, an error occurred during the transfer. You don't have to act on this error! The administration office will automatically receive a message and they will contact you if anything seems wrong. In most cases, this icon only indicates errors that are excepted

Add or detract bonus points

Step 1 Go to the Final Grades Menu and open the Grade Calculation Setup
 
Step 2 Navigate to the Options column
 
  
Step 3 Click on None and select Bonus/malus
 


Instead of having a grade included in the average, you can also set it to count towards the final grade in another way, e.g. as a bonus on top of the average. If you tick the box for the bonus/malus option, you are presented with three options.

  • Only count if it improves the grade
  • Add to calculated average as bonus
  • Subtract from calculated average as malus

Finally, by entering weighting factors (in the 'Weight’ column), you can determine the weight of the various components towards the final grade.

There is no fixed format for setting the weighting factors. The grade calculation module adds up all weighting factors that you have entered and then divides each factor by the sum. For example, it does not matter whether you enter the factors 25 and 75 (as percentages of the whole) or 1 and 3 (as ratios) for two assignments; in both cases, the result will be the same. Below the weighting factors is the total weight, i.e. the sum of the factors that you have entered. This sum allows you to verify that what you have entered is correct.

Please note: if the sum of the factors should be something other than 100%, e.g. because part of the final grade is determined by attendance at seminars or active participation in discussions, you can set this option by splitting a component into sub-components and/or selecting the bonus/malus option for one or more components

Add new calculated item

For advanced calculations, you combine assignments in groups so as to release a calculation around it. This is comparable with Assignment Groups in Canvas, however, the Final Grades tool allows more complex calculation options. A calculated item can be recognized by a blue link whereas regular assignments are displayed in plain text.

Every time you need to setup a certain calculation around one or more assignments, you first create a calculated item to which you can then move sub-assignments. For example: the final grade could be based on the weighted average of an exam on the one hand and a set of assignments on the other. In this case you'll need to setup a system so the separate grades for each of the assignments are weighted as one component.

The process of adding calculated items allows you to create various component "layers", so to speak. There is no limit on the number of calculated items to use (each calculated item can contain multiple new calculated components, which in turn can contain calculated components of their own, and so on).

Step 2 Click on the top right button Add partial result and select New calculated item

When to use a scaled (1-10) grade (cesuur)

Introduction
 

At the UvA, most teachers score a 1 out of 10, when students earned 0 points for their exam. In one sense, the other 9 points are divided over the questions. For basic explanation purposes, here are three examples of when to use a cesuur. 
In its most basic form, teachers must decide:

- Do students receive a 5,0 if they score half the points?

- Do students receive a 5,5 if they receive half the points?

In sum, the big question is: how will your students' points be converted to a grade? Is it linear? Or is it on a graded scale with a cesuur? 

Need a visual?
No idea what this is all about? Check out the table below with example points and grades on a 15-point scale. 

View example

Example conversion from points to grades (7,5 points would be a 5,5)

 points  linear score graded score with cesuur difference
1 0,7 1,6 0,9
2 1,3 2,2 0,9
3 2 2,8 0,8
4 2,7 3,4 0,7
5 3,3 4 0,7
6 4 4,6 0,6
7 4,7 5,2 0,5
8 5,3 5,8 0,5
9 6 6,4 0,4
10 6,7 7 0,3
11 7,3 7,6 0,3
12 8 8,2 0,2
13 8,7 8,8 0,1
14 9,3 9,4 0,1
15 10 10 0

 
 

 
Example 1 Testing with an essay/take-home exam 
  Teachers grade from 1-10. They decide when something is a 5,5 or not, therefore, no cesuur settings are needed.
 
Example 2 Testing with Multiple choice questions
 

When Canvas Quizzes are used, the Final grades tool will automatically let half the points score a 5,0. 
0 points will then also be a '0' rather than a '1'. Therefore, we recommend that tests with MC questions use the built in cesuur settings in the Final grades tool. 

 
Example 3 Testing with open ended questions that were too difficult
  It is also possible that your exam was too difficult. You think it fair to give students some leeway by applying the cesuur settings to your exam. 
 
Example 4 You want to use your own cesuur (advanced)
 

This is not supported, but since we are in the advanced section: You may also get your personal cesuur via https://cijfersberekenen.nl/
You must follow these steps:

  • Download your students points using the import/export tool
  • Convert your points via an Excel, using Vlookup and apply your cesuur from https://cijfersberekenen.nl/ to the desired grade
  • Upload the grade to Canvas using the import/export tool

Preview grades before publishing

It is suggested you also check a few students’ grades before publishing. Mistakes are easily made, especially with more complicated calculation setups.

Step 1 Go to the Final Grades Menu 
   
Step 2 Click on Recalculate grades at the top right and select Recalculate ALL grades 
 

Step 3 Scroll down to the list with students on the left
   
Step 4 Select one student (at random) by clicking on the 'down' arrow at the right
 

The grade calculation of this student now unfolds


This overview contains the final grade , the final grade after retake(s) , and grade details of all first attempts . More details on (partial) retake grades can be found under the Open grade calculation button at the bottom of this list).

 
Tip!   

Tip: If your calculation setup contains a Retake, try to select a student who has done the retake to double check this element of the calculation.

Publish grades

Step 1 Go to the Final Grades Menu 
   
Step 2 Click on Grade Calculation at the right and select Publish calculated grades 
 

Step 3 Select all assignments
 

Simply press the top check box and all the subitems will also be checked.




Step 4 Click OK
  Now the grades are published to students

Step 5 How to inform students
 

Please note : Students have to be informed how they can look up their grades. This is explained below .

Please inform students to:

      1. Head over to ‘Grades’

      2. Press on the ‘Show final grade’ button at the top right

      3. Students will now see all their grades as was previewed in the Open grade calculation .
