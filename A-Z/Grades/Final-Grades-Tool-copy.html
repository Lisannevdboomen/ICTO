About Final Grades

From 2021/22 onwards, the Final grades tool saves you a lot of time when submitting grades to SIS. You can use the Final Grades menu to add grades, use a variety of calculation options, and to publish the final grade breakdown to students and the SiS Administration

Add or detract points

1. Go to the Final Grades menu and open the grade Calculation Setup
 

Can't see this course menu?

  1. Go to Settings at the bottom of your course menu

  2. Click on the tab Navigation at the top

  3. Scroll down until you reach the Final Grades menu. Enable this menu by clicking on the icon with three dots or by dragging and drop this menu to the top.

  4. Click on Save at the bottom of the page
2. Navigate to the Options column

 
3. Click on None and select Bonus/malus
 


Instead of having a grade included in the average, you can also set it to count towards the final grade in another way, e.g. as a bonus on top of the average.

If you tick the box for the bonus/malus option, you are presented with three options:

    • Only count if it improves the grade

The item will only count if it improves the grade. If it doesn't, the item won't be included in the calculated average.

    • Add to calculated average as bonus

Add (unweighted) result of this item to final grade.

    • Subtract from calculated average as malus

Subtract the (unweighted) result of this item from final grade.


Finally, by entering weighting factors (in the 'Weight’ column), you can determine the weight of the various components towards the final grade.

There is no fixed format for setting the weighting factors. The grade calculation module adds up all weighting factors that you have entered and then divides each factor by the sum. For example, it does not matter whether you enter the factors 25 and 75 (as percentages of the whole) or 1 and 3 (as ratios) for two assignments; in both cases, the result will be the same. Below the weighting factors is the total weight, i.e. the sum of the factors that you have entered. This sum allows you to verify that what you have entered is correct.

Please note: if the sum of the factors should be something other than 100%, e.g. because part of the final grade is determined by attendance at seminars or active participation in discussions, you can set this option by splitting a component into sub-components and/or selecting the bonus/malus option for one or more components

Add cesuur

 

Introduction
 

When grading exams or assignments, a decision must be made on how to convert the obtained points into a grade. This can be done either in a linear way, or by adding a caesura (cesuur).

In its most basic form, this means that teachers must decide:

- Do students receive a 5,0 if they score half the points? (linear)

- Do students receive a 5,5 if they receive half the points? (cesuur)

Need a visual?
No idea what this is all about? Check out the table below with example points and grades on a 15-point scale.

How to convert total points of an exercise to a 10 point scale

 

Introduction
 

In some cases, you may have an assignment where there are more than 10 points to be gained. Be it with a quiz where you can get a total of 40 points, or with an assignment where you obtain a grade using a unique scoring system or rubric. Anyway, by creating a calculated item you can easily convert the score of an assignment to 10 points.

In this tutorial we will use the example of an exam where the total score of 50 points needs to be converted to 10 points. You can follow the example using your own total score.

The following two steps will be covered:

  1. We are going to place the exam in a calculated item
  2. We are going to convert the total points of the exam to 10 points

 
1. Go to the Final Grades menu and open the grade Calculation Setup
 

Can't see this course menu?

  1. Go to Settings at the bottom of your course menu

  2. Click on the tab Navigation at the top

  3. Scroll down until you reach the Final Grades menu. Enable this menu by clicking on the icon with three dots or by dragging and drop this menu to the top.

  4. Click on Save at the bottom of the page
2. Click on Add partial result and select New calculated item

 

 
3. Insert a title for the calculated item and click OK

 

 
4. Now the calculated item appears in the setup. Click on the blue title to visit this item.

 

 
5. You've entered the calculated item. Click on Add partial result and select Existing assignment

You can also select New Canvas Assignment in case the assignment(s) you need do not exist yet.




 

 
6. Select the assignments that you would like to add and click OK.

 

 
7. Now the assignments are lined up in the calculated item.



Click on Final grade if you would like to go back to the main setup



Notice that the calculated item is now filled with 6 partial results

3. Select a student (at random) from the student list by clicking the 'down' arrow at the right side

Publish grades

1. Go to the Final Grades menu
 

Can't see this course menu?

  1. Go to Settings at the bottom of your course menu

  2. Click on the tab Navigation at the top

  3. Scroll down until you reach the Final Grades menu. Enable this menu by clicking on the icon with three dots or by dragging and drop this menu to the top.

  4. Click on Save at the bottom of the page
2. Click on Grade calculation and select Publish calculated grades
 

 
3. Select all assigments and/or exams
 

Pressing the top checkbox will automatically check all subitems.


 

Publishing a partial grade:

To publish a partial grade, only select the assigment or exam you want the grade(s) to be published for (and deselect 'Final Grade').

 

Publishing a retake grade:

Select the assignment/exam you wish to publish a retake grade for and select the Include retake grades option.

 
4. Click OK
  The grades will now be published to students.
Please note that students are not automatically informed by this.

 
5. How to inform students
 

Please inform students to:

  1. Head over to 'Grades'

  2. Click on the Show final grade button at the top right

  3. All grades will now be visible

Monitor grades after publishing

Once all grades have been published, we recommend you check if the grades are visible to students in the way you had in mind.
To do so, please do the following:

  • Go to the homepage of the course and select Student view
  • Go to the Grades menu and click on the Show final grade button.
  • By clicking Pick... you can select a student and check if the grades are shown as expected.
