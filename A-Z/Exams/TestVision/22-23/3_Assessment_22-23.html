1.1   How to grade in TestVision

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: Only open questions need to be graded, as multiple choice questions are graded automatically.

1. Go to the Grading menu.
 

Grading menu

2. Go to the Grader tab and click on Grade for the desired exam.
  The grader tab will display the exams to which you've been assigned as grader. Don't see your exam(s)?


3. Start grading by clicking on Grade next to the desired student.
 

You can also choose to grade by question, by clicking on the Grade per question tab.
4. Assign points per criterion.
  When you're done grading a question, click on Next to go the next question (or student, if you're grading per question).

1.3   Adjust the number of points a question is worth

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Warning: Adjusting the score of criteria will erase all given points for those criteria! It's strongly advised to make any adjustments before grading.

1. Go to the Grading menu.
 

Grading menu

2. Go to the Grader tab and click on Grade.
  The grader tab will display the exams to which you've been assigned as grader. Don't see your exam(s)?


3. Start grading by clicking on Grade next to the desired student.
 

You can also choose to grade by question, by clicking on the Grade per question tab.
 

 
4. Click on the Modify criteria button to start adjusting your criteria.
  A new window will open in which you can modify the text and amount of points per criterion.
5. Click on the Adjust scoring button to confirm that you wish to change the scores.
 

Warning: Adjusting the score of criteria will erase all given points for those criteria!

You can also modify the description of criteria without erasing any points by clicking on the criterion.
 

 
6. A warning will show which says all registered scores will be erased once the score of a criterion is adjusted. Click on OK to proceed.
  This warning will only show if you've started grading students already.

7. You can now adjust the scoring. Click on a criterion's number of points to adjust it.
  The original criteria descriptions and points are shown on the right. Click on OK to confirm your changes.
8. Your changes have been saved.
  You can now grade according to your newly adjusted criteria.
Unfortunately, TestVision does not offer any videos on this topic.

2.1   Analyse exam results

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: You can only analyse the results once they have been validated.

Warning: A reliable analysis requires at least 100 exam results.

1. Go to the Tests menu.
 

Tests menu

2. Click on the ∑-icon.
 

3. Click on the Analysis button.
 

4. The Test Analysis tab shows a general analysis of the exam.
 

You can hover your mouse over terms to show an explanation.
5. The Item Analysis tab shows a breakdown per question.
  Question quality is indicated with colours. TestVision uses the p-value, p'-value, a-values and rit-value to determine the quality of question.
- Green indicates that the above values satisfy the most important quality standards.
- Orange indicates that one or more values somewhat or seriously deviate from the quality standards.
- Red indicates various values don't satisfy quality standards.
Please visit the TestVision documentation (NL/ENG) to find out how the question quality is calculated.

In this menu, you can also hover your mouse over column names to show an explanation.
6. The Frequency Distribution tab shows the distribution of scores.
  If the frequency distribution graph does not approximate a bell curve, the exam might have been been too difficult or easy.

2.2   Mark questions as correct

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: You can only mark questions as correct once the exam has been validated.

1. Go to the Tests menu.
 

Tests menu

2. Click on the ∑-icon.
 

3. Click on the Modify button.
 

4. Go to the Item result tab to see an overview of the questions.
  In this menu, you can mark answers as correct, exclude them or modify them.
5. Select the questions you'd like you to mark as correct and click on Check modifications.
  You can mark multiple questions correct at once or go one by one and see how the results are affected after you've clicked Check modifications.
6. An overview will show how the results are affected. Click Apply modifications to confirm.
  An overview with three columns will show you how the results are affected by your modifications:
- Original shows what the results were like after the test was taken, before any modifications.
- Current shows what the results are like currently. The original and current columns will be the same if no modifications were done yet.
- New shows what the results will look like after you apply your changes.

2.3   Mark specific answers as correct for all students (modify answer key)

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: You can only modify the answer key once the exam has been validated.

1. Go to the Tests menu.
 

Tests menu

2. Click on the ∑-icon.
 

3. Click on the Modify button.
 

4. Go to the Item result tab to see an overview of the questions.
  In this menu, you can mark answers as correct, exclude them or modify them.
5. Click the modify icon for the question you'd like to modify.
  Every question type will have a different type of modification. You can scroll through the most common ones below or click on the names to navigate to them:
- Multiple choice
- Open questions
- Fill in the blank

6A.		 Multiple choice questions:
 

Note: You can only mark one additional answer as correct. Unfortunately, there's no way to mark multiple answers as correct (besides marking the entire question as correct).

Select the option you wish to mark as correct. If you wish to mark both answers as correct, make sure to check the Highest result checkbox. Click on OK to confirm your modifications.

6B.		 Open questions:
 

Note: To mark answers of individual students as correct, please go to 2.4 Regrade questions for individual students.

Select the criteria you'd like to mark as correct and click on OK to confirm your modifications. To quickly view the question and criteria again, click on the info icon and on Edit.

6C.		 Fill in the blank:
  All given answers graded as incorrect will be shown with the frequency in between brackets. Select all answers you wish to mark as correct and click on OK to confirm your modifications. Contrary to multiple choice questions, there is no limit to how many answers you can mark as correct.
 

 
7. Click on Check modifications to view the effect of your modifications.
  The modify icon will turn green for questions of which the answer key has been modified. Click on Check modifications to see how the results are affected.
8. An overview will show how the results are affected. Click Apply modifications to confirm.
  An overview with three columns will show you how the results are affected by your modifications:
- Original shows what the results were like after the test was taken, before any modifications.
- Current shows what the results are like currently. The original and current columns will be the same if no modifications were done yet.
- New shows what the results will look like after you apply your changes.

2.4   Regrade questions for individual students

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: You can only modify the answer key once the exam has been validated.

1. Go to the Sessions menu.
 

Sessions menu

2. Click on the Results button.
 

3. Select the student you want to regrade and click on the Modify button.
  The results are sorted alphabetically by student name by default. You can sort them by other columns by clicking on the column name.
4. You can mark a question as correct, exclude it or regrade an open question.
  You can scroll through the options below or click on the names to navigate to them:
- Mark questions as correct
- Regrade open question

5A.		 Mark questions as correct for an individual student:
  Select the questions you wish to mark as correct. You can see how the scoring is affected in the score column.

5B.		 Regrade an open question for an individual student:
  Click on the modify icon. Enter the new score for the student and click on OK to confirm your modifications. The original score is shown in between brackets. Below, the original score per criterion are shown, with the max. amount of point in between brackets.
6. Click on Check modifications to view the effect of your modifications.
  The score of an open question will turn green if it has been modified.
7. An overview will show how the score of the student is affected. Click on OK to confirm the new score.
  The original score is shown on the right side.

2.6   Modify grade calculation

  • Step-by-step
  • Video
TestVision Help: ENG | NL

 

Note: You can only change the grade calculation once the exam has been validated.

1. Go to the Tests menu.
 

Tests menu

2. Click on the ∑-icon.
 

3. Click on the Modify button.
 

4. Go to the Test result tab to view the grade calculation.
  In this menu you can adjust how the points are converted to grades.
5. Modify the grade calculation to your liking.
  You can configure the percentage of correct answers that belongs to a 1.0, 5.5 and a 10. The graph will show the grade conversion for all percentages. In the screenshot below:
- Scoring 10% or less will result in a 1.0.
- Scoring 55% will result in a 5.5.
- Scoring 100% will result in a 10.

Note: To reproduce grade = score / maximum score * 9 + 1, configure your grade calculation as follows:
  • 0% → 1.0
  • 50% → 5.5
  • 100% → 10
6. Click on Check modifications.
 

7. An overview will show how the results are affected. Click Apply modifications to confirm.
  An overview with three columns will show you how the results are affected by your modifications:
- Original shows what the results were like after the test was taken, before any modifications.
- Current shows what the results are like currently. The original and current columns will be the same if no modifications were done yet.
- New shows what the results will look like after you apply your changes.

3A. Export grades to Canvas via Excel

TestVision Help: ENG | NL

Note: The regional format of your computer must be set to the same language as TestVision for this tutorial. If your computer's regional format does not match TestVision's language, the grade export from TestVision will be interpreted incorrectly and as a result the grades cannot be exported to Canvas.

1. Ensure the regional format of your computer to the same language as TestVision.
  You can set your regional format of your PC under Windows icon > Settings > Time & Languague > Region > Regional Format. Select English (United States) if TestVision is set to English. Select Dutch (Netherlands) if TestVision is set to Dutch. This tutorial uses English (United States), as TestVision is set to English.

How to navigate to the regional format settings







2. Open TestVision and go to the Sessions menu.
 

Sessions menu



3. Go to the Results of the exam.
 

4. An overview of the grades for this exam will be shown. Click on Download to download the grades.
  You can also sort the grades by clicking on the header of the 'Result' column.
5. Select Download results as CSV.
  The results will be downloaded as a comma-separated values file to your computer's Downloads folder.
6. Open the file you just downloaded and rename the 'mark' (or 'cijfer' if TestVision is set to Dutch) column to the name you want your students to see in Canvas when you post the grades.
  If you wish, you can delete all columns except those with the student ID's and grades, as only these are required for the import.
7. Save the file as an Excel workbook (.xlsx) file.
  A new .xlsx file will be created in the same location on your computer. You can rename the file if you wish.

How to save a file as an Excel workbook (.xlsx)





8. Go to the final grades tool in Canvas and click on Import from file > Partial grades.
 

9.

A new window will open. Select and match the student ID and grade columns for import.
  Please ensure the following:
  1. loginname (or aanmeldnaam if TestVision was set to Dutch) is set to <Student ID>.
  2. The grade column you just renamed is set to <new component>. You can also select an existing Canvas assignment if you've already created an assignment.
Don't see your Canvas assignment?
Don't see you Canvas assignment?
If you want to upload the grades to an existing Canvas assignment through the final grades tool, make sure you've added your existing Canvas assignment to the final grades tool calculation setup before importing the grades. Learn how to add your assignment to the calculation here.



Dutch import settings





10.

Scroll down and click on OK to import the grades.
  Select the mute newly created assignment option if you don't want the grades to be posted yet. This option is only available if you're creating new assignments (<new component>). After pressing OK, your grades have been imported to Canvas! You can view them in the gradebook and in the final grades tool after calculating the grades.
11. Click on Recalculate grades > Calculate all grades to calculate the final grades based on the grades you just imported.
  All grades shown in this menu are not visible to students, so don't be afraid to try and play around with the calculation.

3B. Direct grade export to Canvas (with chance score enabled and fixed grade calculation)

Note: If you choose to export your grades using this method, the grades in the final grades tool might differ from those in TestVision. This method extracts the points from TestVision and applies its own grade calculation to these. This calculation always includes a chance score for multiple choice questions, which unfortunately cannot be disabled. If you wish to export your grades without a chance score, please disable the chance score and export the grades via Excel.

1. Go to the Calculation setup in Canvas' Final grades menu.
  Can't find the final grades menu? You'll have to enable it first.





2. Click on + Add partial result to add the TestVision exam to the calculation.
 


3. Select the exam and click on OK.
  The exam is listed underneath a header called TestVision Online. The exam can also be recognized by the same [date] - [exam type] - [course name] - [course code] format and name as the exam in TestVision.
4. The exam has now been added to the calculation.
  You can enter the desired weights and further configure your calculation if desired.
5. Click on Retrieve TestVision grades.
  This button retrieves the latest version of the grades from TestVision. If you change the grades in TestVision later, you'll have to retrieve the grades again using this same button.
6. A new window will open to monitor the progress of retrieving the grades from TestVision.
  Click on Close once the latest status message is "All done".
7. Click on Recalculate grades > Calculate all grades to calculate the final grades based on the grades you just retrieved.
  All grades shown in this menu are not visible to students, so don't be afraid to try and play around with the calculation.
