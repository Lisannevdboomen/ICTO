1. Upload the studyguide

One of the most important things is to share a study guide (course syllabus) so that students are aware of the course program and schedule. We advise you to use Canvas for this purpose. It is helpful to place the study guide in a prominent place, somewhere not to be missed.

Teachers commonly use one of the two options below


1. Upload the study guide to a course home page

First, create a new page. Add some text and upload the study guide onto the page.

2. Upload the study guide to Modules

Go to Modules in your course menu. Create a new module by clicking on the upper right button + Module.

Now, add the study guide to this module. You can do this by clicking on the upper right button + within the module and by adding the guide as a "file" from your local computer.

2. Organize your Canvas course

Education largely takes place online, ensuring a good online learning environment is therefore more important than ever. In the next steps of this instruction we will come to the technical "musts", but for now, we will discuss some helpful tips to keep your canvas course neat and organized. This is not only great for students, but also for yourself and your team!

Use the course menu Modules as your leading course environment

You can make use of so many options and features in Canvas. Be it a Page with some introductionary text, an Assignment where students can hand in their paper, or a File containing literature. For every option there's a different menu to store these items in. However, there's one exception! If you're using the course menu Modules, you can actually import and combine all these elements together to create a clear overview. Instead of students having to click through different kinds of course menus and items, you can now provide a structured and powerfull setup.

How to use Modules in your Canvas course



Minimize your course menu

For each course, there are at least 20 different kinds of menus to make use of, you are probably not going to use all of these. We strongly recommend that you hide any parts you're not using, otherwise students aren't able to properly navigate the course content.

3. About Zoom & Livestreaming

At the UvA, we use Zoom to allow seminar groups and lectures to take place online. In addition, you can use livestreaming devices (the BRIO or OWL) to better capture the sound and environment of seminar rooms and lecture halls. The combination of on-site and online education is commonly called "hybrid education". For more information on online tools and hybrid resources, visit this comprehensive page.

The Brio is a webcam with an exceptionally good microphone, which has been installed in all seminar rooms and lecture halls. The BRIO functions well together with Zoom, serving as a livestream for your classes and lectures. Note: some departments have furnished a few seminar rooms in a different way, but these are rare exceptions. If in doubt, look at rooster.uva.nl to see if there's a 'Zoom' tag connected to the room, or contact your timetable manager. More Information offered by the Audio Visual Service of the REC.

You do not need to book or reserve the BRIO

  • You just need to know how to start/connect the device to a laptop or computer:
    • Use the instruction sheet present in lecture halls and seminar rooms
    • Ask a student assistant for help
    • Start-up support is available on request or ad-hoc in case of AV issues via the Facility Service Desk, (020 525) 1403 or servicedesk-fs@uva.nl

The BRIO will be used in combination with Zoom

    1. Create a Zoom meeting in your Canvas course. This can be one recurring Zoom meeting for all lectures or separate meetings.
    2. Tell students to head over to the Zoom menu in Canvas to access the live lecture.
    3. The livestream will happen over Zoom. By using a BRIO, the lecture/seminar hall is now better captured in terms of audio and video quality.

4. Publish your course

Every aspect of Canvas works on a "Publish" and "Unpublish" basis. Want to put your Canvas course live? Or share a page with students? Publish it! Manage the visibility of specific content by using the publish and unpublish button and use "Student View" if you want to double check.

Double check with Student View

Student view allows teachers to view a Canvas course as their students would. This is a useful tool to double check if your course is set up properly (e.g. to check if the aspects of your course that you wish to be published, are actually visible to students).

Navigate to your course's homepage and click the Student view button at the top right.

Make sure to check if:

  • The correct course menus at the left are visble to students.
    Missing something? Exit 'Student view' and navigate to: 'Settings' > 'Navigation'. Make items visible by dragging them from the bottom to the top list.

  • The correct modules are published, along with their corresponding content.

6. Create Assignments or Exams

There are two ways to create online exams: with TestVision or with Canvas. If you are going to create an online exam that will be administered on site, please note that you can only use TestVision for this purpose. Contact your study programme coordinator or manager to discuss which option(s) apply to your study programme.

Canvas Exams

 

 
  • Canvas Quizzes
  • Canvas Assignments

See how you can create exams using Canvas. You can use Canvas Quizzes for closed-ended questions and Canvas Assignments for open-ended questions and take home exams. Use our practical tips to reduce the risk of fraud and share our instructions to students so they are well-informed and prepared before starting the exam.

TestVision

 

 
  • TestVision
  • Digial examination software

TestVision is a digital examination application for exams and depends more on ICTO. Compared to creating exams on Canvas, you will need a little more time to learn how to work with TestVision, but there are certainly more options and settings at your disposal to create strong exams and to review and adjust the quality of questions. Visit our TestVision page to become familiar with the overall creation process, and when you're ready, contact icto if you'd like to create an exam with TestVision.

7. Manage grades

All assignments and grades in Canvas can be found in the Grades menu, often called 'the Gradebook'. Additionally, starting this year, a new menu called Final Grades has been added to Canvas that allows you to calculate grades to a final grade. Teachers are expected to push final grades from Canvas to SIS via this new menu (using Two-Factor Authentication).

Grades menu

The Gradebook (Grades) shows all your assignments and assessments within Canvas. You can use the gradebook to assess and grade the work of students. How do I use canvas Grades?

 

Final Grades menu

Final Grades allows you to use grades from Canvas and/or Excel and calculate and submit final grades from Canvas to SIS. How do I use canvas Final Grades?
