The Thesis Workflow allows students to easily publish their Master Thesis to the UvA Library within Canvas. The workflow consists of a few modules that students must carefully read and follow one by one to complete the workflow. By using this structure, students are well informed about the decisions they need to make, such as which creative commons license to choose or what the official title will be for the published version. By following the workflow module by module, students can submit their thesis according to their own needs and decisions, and their request will be automatically forwarded to the UvA library. Please read our description below to find out the do's and don'ts in order for the workflow to (technically) work.

Checklist for teachers / administrators

You are welcome to edit due dates of the assignments.

Please keep the Modules menu open and visible. This way, students can start off with the first assignment of the workflow and click “next” to follow the process. Try to close the Assignments menu to prevent confusion for students.

Please do not create any alternate/substitute assignments. Students will have to submit their thesis in the workflow assignment inbox that we imported to your course.

Please do not change the titles of the assignments and modules, otherwise it will not be possible to upload a thesis to the UvA Library.

Noticed the strange titles above each assignment (in the Assignments menu) such as “reporting.id.concept”? These titles should not be edited as well.

 

Canvas view for students

1. Students start with submitting the thesis title for their diploma supplement

2. Then there are one or two surveys/quizzes, depending on whether a student wants to publish their thesis in the UvA Library or not

3. After that they can upload their thesis in PDF

4. Finally there's a webpage to notify them that they've successfully uploaded their thesis.
