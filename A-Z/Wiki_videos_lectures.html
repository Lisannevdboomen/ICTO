Weblecture recordings

    • Teachers can request one or several recordings with http://webcollegeplanner.uva.nl/. For more questions about weblectures, please contact webcolleges@uva.nl (T: 020 525 2800).
    • What functionalities can I request with the webcollegeplanner?

      Options

      What it does

      on demand

      Recording: The web link to the recording will be sent in advance, it still needs to be shared with students.

      live

      Live & Recording: A stream link and recording link will be sent in advance. The link must still be shared with students.

      only audio

      Sound Recording: Sometimes you just want an audio recording without image/video.

      forward

      Forward to other room: Allows you to stream to another room.
    • It is possible to request a livestream with the webcollegeplanner. However, the livestream function has a delay of about 30 seconds which makes it not ideal for communicating with students at home. Starting this academic year, there will be a BRIO attached in every lecture hall by default and this will allow you to achieve the same goal, only without delay. Go to the 'Livestream' section at the top of this page to read more about it.

Communicating with students at home (during the lecture)

    • How do you ensure that students can ask questions from home during a lecture? The following option is identified by teachers as most helpful and (above else) the easiest: ask a student or student-assistant onsite to keep track of questions in the Zoom chat. Teaching is hard enough these days, luckily we notice students are very willing to help.
    • Use our new licensed tool WooClap to interact with students online.
    • Really looking for something other than the Zoom chat or WooClap? Although we do not recommend using unlicensed tools, well.. head over to 'Interactive tools' at the top of this page and look for 'Padlet', you might like it!
    • Due to the limitations of classroom sound (no microphone to pick up students' voices), it is important for the instructor to repeat students' questions and comments so that online students are aware of the conversation on campus.

Finding the right format for your hybrid classes

    • While we are most certainly excited for our on-campus days to return, realistically, we also know that there are likely to be some “post-pandemic” situations that necessitate some flexibility in teaching. The FMG-Hybrid Committee has worked with the TLC, ICTO, and FS-BOL to ensure that you have the information and technology you need to make flexible teaching accessible to you. Since we know that classes, content, and teachers are different, the options are also quite varied. With that in mind, on this website, you can find flexible teaching options grouped into three general formats.

BRIO livestreaming device

    • The Brio is a webcam with an exceptionally good microphone, which has been installed in the lecture halls and which can be reserved for use in a seminar room from the REC Facility Services (soon, the BRIO will be installed in seminar rooms too). The BRIO functions well together with Zoom, serving as a livestream for your classes and lectures. More Information offered by the Audio Visual Service of the REC.

    • You do not need to reserve the BRIO for lecture halls

    • You just need to know how to start/connect the device to your computer:
      • Use the instruction sheet present in lecture halls
      • Ask a student assistant for help
      • Start-up support is available on request or ad-hoc in case of AV issues via the Facility Service Desk, (020 525) 1403 or servicedesk-fs@uva.nl

    • The BRIO will be used in combination with Zoom

      1. Create a Zoom meeting in your Canvas course. This can be one recurring Zoom meeting for all lectures or separate meetings.
      2. Tell students to head over to the Zoom menu in Canvas to access the live lecture.
      3. The livestream will happen over Zoom. By using a BRIO, the lecture/seminar hall is now better captured in terms of audio and video quality.

    • At least one week in advance, this equipment can be booked (for seminar rooms) via the Facility Services Service Desk, (020 525) 1403 or servicedesk-fs@uva.nl.

OWL livestreaming device

    • The OWL is a device that provides online students a 360-degree viewing experience of the classroom (via a 360-degree camera) and allows students to hear the lecturer as it also functions as in-classroom microphone. This device is particularly suitable for group discussions, more speakers are automatically visible at the same time (Split screen). More Information offered by the Audio Visual Service of the REC.

    • At least one week in advance, teachers can request to use the OWLS by emailing servicedesk-fs@uva.nl (020 525) 1403 and/or working with their teaching planner. The device will be brought to the classroom at the scheduled time.

    • Start-up support is available on request or ad-hoc in case of AV issues via the Facility Service Desk, (020 525) 1403 or servicedesk-fs@uva.nl.

    • The OWL will be used in combination with Zoom

      1. Create a Zoom meeting in your Canvas course. This can be one recurring Zoom meeting for all lectures or separate meetings.
      2. Tell students to head over to the Zoom menu in Canvas to access the live lecture.
      3. The livestream will happen over Zoom. By using the OWL, the lecture/seminar hall is now better captured in terms of audio and video quality.

Hybrid Learning Theatre

    • With professional audiovisual support and a unique atmosphere, this space offers an exceptional experience for hybrid teaching.
    • Time slots are available every day from 08:30-22:00. It is not only possible to have lectures in the theater but also Q&A sessions, seminars/tutorials, student presentations or other types of activities. There can be 13 students invited for physical attendance at a time but it is not mandatory.
