Assignments

 

 
  • Assignment settings
  • Options & features
  • Exams

Assignments in Canvas can be used to challenge students' understanding and help assess competency. Create a space where students can submit their documents or video files. There are a variety of options, settings and tools to use such as Peer Reviews, Group Assignments, Rubrics, and external tools (LTI) like Perusall and FeedbackFruits.

Plagiarism

 

 
  • Turnitin Similarity
  • Plagiarism score
  • Score breakdown

Turnitin Similarity is the plagiarism software of the UvA. It compares student submissions with existing texts of various different sources. The degree of similarities with other texts online, or with other student submissions, are presented with a similarity percentage.

Perusall

 

 
  • Perusall
  • Assignments
  • Deep reading

In Perusall students can read, comment on and discuss on texts (e.g. articles and book chapters) you upload. They see the comments of other students and this way they can ask each other questions, discuss the text, and link to other texts. For the teacher Perusall produces a report to indicate where in the text students had most questions or discussion.

Rubrics

 

 
  • Feedback & Review
  • Graded/Ungraded
  • Free rubrics

A Rubric is an assessment tool for communicating expectations of quality. Rubrics are typically comprised of rows and columns. Rows are used to define the various criteria being used to assess an assignment. Columns are used to define levels of performance for each criterion.

Peer Review

 

 

  • Peer review settings
  • automatic vs. manually

When creating an assignment, you can require students to complete a peer review of another student's work. The peer review system allows students to annotate, comment on and assess the work of fellow students. Tip: add rubrics to the peer review system to improve the quality of feedback and reviewing skills of students.

Grades

 

 
  • Gradebook
  • Post grades

The Gradebook helps instructors easily view and enter grades for students. Depending on the Grade display type, grades for each assignment can be viewed as points, percentage, complete or incomplete, GPA scale, or letter grade. Only graded assignments, graded discussions, graded quizzes, and graded surveys that have been published display in the Gradebook. Not Graded assignments are not included.

 

 View more

Final Grades Tool

 

 
  • SiS Administration
  • Submit grades
  • Calculation settings

The Final Grades Tool offers lecturers a faster and easier way to submit grades from Canvas to SIS using Two-Factor Authentication. It also allows you to calculate Final grades from different (Canvas) elements. Setup your grade calculation using grades from Canvas Assignments, Quizzes, Grades, Excel Imports, et cetera. Then determine your students' final grades using the possibilities of the Final grades tool's options and features.

  • Software

ATLAS.ti is a powerful workbench for the qualitative analysis of large bodies of textual, graphical, audio and video data. Sophisticated tools help you to arrange, reassemble, and manage your material in creative, yet systematic ways. ATLAS.ti keeps you focused on the material itself. Whether your field is anthropology, economics, criminology, or medicine: ATLAS.ti will meet your qualitative analysis needs

From simple forms to randomized studies, with Qualtrics you can create online surveys and give them advanced functionality and a professional look and feel. Set triggers and create 'actions' to distribute your survey. The Data & Analysis tab lets you filter, classify, merge, clean, and statistically analyze your response data.

Wooclap

 

 
  • Wooclap
  • Live Polling

Wooclap is an 'Audience Response Tool'. While teaching/presenting, you can use this tool to present questions or statements to your audience. Attendees can respond to these using their smartphone or laptop. The results can be displayed directly on the screen of the presenter.
