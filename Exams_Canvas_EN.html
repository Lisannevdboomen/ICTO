To make an exam in Canvas a number of steps have to be taken. For example, you have to consider what type of questions you are going to ask. 

In the menu on the right these steps are outlined for you. Click on a step to find out more about it.

Inspection session

Option 1     Close-Ended Questions

Let students answer close-ended questions online for a short period of time (just like an examination).

Pros and cons

  

  • Grading time is minimal

  

  • In order to keep the risk of fraud to a minimum, it is important to be careful when making this test (make good use of our advice & tips).

Option 1 can be combined with Option 2.

 

. .     .      .     . .     .      .    .     .      .     ..

Responsibilities teacher

Support by the examiner during this test is necessary . The coordinating lecturer and/or examiner have to guide students with issues during the test and  grant extra time online if necessary.

Option 2     Open-Ended Questions

Let students answer open-ended questions online for a short period of time (just like an examination).  .    .      .    .

Pros and cons

  

  • Possibility to combine with multiple choice questions
  • Plagiarism check recommended
  • Grading with Rubrics

  

  • More grading required than Option 1
  • In order to prevent fraud, you can opt for a tighter time limit, but this limits the theoretical depth.

Option 2 can be combined with Option 1.

 

Responsibilities teacher

Support by the examiner during this test is necessary. The coordinating lecturer and/or examiner has to be available during the test to guide students with content-related questions, or uploading issues with their answer sheets.

Option 3     Take Home Exam

Give students an examination with a deadline (more than three hours later) on which they can work in their own time.

Pros and cons

  

  • More theoretical depth possible in questions (deeper understanding, application, analyzing, evaluating)
  • Risk of fraud is minimal
  • Plagiarism check recommended

  

  • More grading required

 

 

 

Responsibilities teacher

Some support is necessary during the test: de coordinating teacher and/or examiner can expect questions from students about the take home exam over mail (content-related questions about the test).

How do I prepare close-ended questions in Canvas?

Try to reduce the risk of fraud

An online exam requires knowledge of a higher order
  •  

    Unaudited, online exams are by definition open book. Nothing prevents students from using resources such as the Internet, textbooks and notes. Your exam must be designed for this. In an unshielded testing environment, the student can share screenshots via whatsapp and other social media, or google. An online exam requires knowledge of a higher order (deeper understanding, application, analyzing, evaluating) and is preferably strictly timed, which makes collaboration and plagiarism unattractive. (source).

Here are some practical tips!

  •  
    1. Use a strict time limit at the exam
    2. Offer questions in a random order
    3. Offer answer options in a random order
    4. Show one question at a time
    5. Students are only allowed to answer the question once, block the question after it has been answered
    6. Make each test unique: create a larger pool of multiple choice questions (e.g. 50 questions in a Question Bank) and let students answer a random combination of these (e.g. 25 questions)
    7. The Question Banks are also important in order to cover all course content in an exam. If you have 4 main topics, then you create 4 Question Banks, so that each student gets the same number of questions about each main topic. 

    You can apply these tips by following the steps below

    4 Next, click on   + Add question bank.
    5 You can now name the question bank.

    Then press enter to save the name.

    6 Open the new question bank by clicking on the name (blue letters).

Step 3                Deadline and time slot

                              How do I set the quiz duration and deadline?

  •  

    Attention! There is a difference between the due date and availability date, read more below

    1 Go to the menu Quizzes.
    2 Click on the exam and edit.
    3 Scroll down. At the last window Assign to everyone we can set the time: 
     

    • Assign to

      By default, this option is set to: Everyone. You can leave it like this.

    • Due date

      This is the deadline/due date of the exam. On this date and time the quiz is concluded for each participant. The hand-in date is also visible during the test.

    • Available: from - to

      This indicates when the exam may appear in the Quizzes menu of the course. Before and after the availability students can not see the exam, only the teachers can see the quiz at that moment. After the 'available until' time the quiz is also concluded for each participant.

    Important!

    • Make sure that the availability (Available From and Until) of the quiz matches the due date : this way latecomers are only allocated the remaining time and the quiz is automatically ended..

Are both time restrictions set to the correct time and date? There is also a Take the quiz button at the bottom of the screen, but this option sometimes does not work properly. We therefore advice you to use the Preview button at the top.
    1 Go to the menu Quizzes.
    2 Click on the quiz.
    3 Check if all settings are correct by going through them one by one..
    4 Click on Preview at your top screen and run through the questions one last time.
    5 If all details are correct, then you can publish the quiz.
      If the start date (available from) is already today and on this time, then keep in mind that the quiz will be directly visible to students! If not, the quiz will only be set up for use but is not yet directly visible to students. In that case, the quiz appears on the time and date according to the settings.
     

    To publish, click on the button Publish:

    6 If you decide to place the quiz in the menu Modules, then do please do not forget to publish the module in which the quiz is places. If the module is not published, students will not see the quiz.
     

Advice and tips!

  • Make sure that the availability (Available From and Until) of the quiz is set equal to the due date: this way students who are late will only be allocated the time remaining.
  • Always create a second quiz for students who are entitled to make the quiz with extra time. If this concerns more than 15 students, then please contact your local ICTO team. Do not forget to edit the duration of the second quiz. 
  • Please do not use open questions in this quiz. It it not possible to add a plagiarism check to a quiz. If you would like to make use of open questions, then please return to our menu at the top of this instruction and go to our instructions about open ended questions and take home exams.
  • Create a practice quiz in Canvas with random sample questions so students can practice with the online environment in advance. Allow students to make this practice quiz more than once (this option can be selected within the quiz settings).
  • If you would like to read more about Canvas Quizzes, then please check: this website

Try to reduce the risk of fraud

An online exam requires knowledge of a higher order
  •  

    Unaudited, online exams are by definition open book. Nothing prevents students from using resources such as the Internet, textbooks and notes. Your exam must be designed for this. In an unshielded testing environment, the student can share screenshots via whatsapp and other social media, or google. An online exam requires knowledge of a higher order (deeper understanding, application, analyzing, evaluating). In addition, the plagiarism check will be available to check for fraud.

    Option 2: exam with open questions

    This exam is preferably strictly timed, which makes collaboration and plagiarism unattractive.

    Option 3: take home exam

    there should be more time available for students to take the test. Consider asking students to create a reference list or bibliography. In addition, the plagiarism check will be available to check for fraud.

    • Part 1 Open questions, Retake 16-03-19 (For composite exams: part 2 would be a multiple choice quiz)

Step 3                Prepare the assignment

                              How can I make the document with questions available during the test?

  •  

    Of course, you can decide for yourself what the document with open questions will look like. We advise you to share the questions in a PDF document, this way you can be sure that all students can open the document during the test.

    1 Go to the menu Files.
    2 Click on + Folder on the top right and add a name

Now go to the right of your screen and click on the Files tab (if you are working on a smaller laptop, then the Files tab will be shown at the very bottom of this page).

                              How do I add rubrics to the assignment?

  •  

    Rubrics can be very helpful when your are grading assignments. Moreover, it also helps students to recognize which parts were good and which elements could be better.

    Make sure that if you want to use a rubric, you set it in advance. This is to avoid complications later on when you are grading the assignments. Also be aware that the rubric will be visible to students.

    1 Go to the menu Assignments.
    2 Click on the assignment.
    3 Click on + Rubric (scroll down below if the button is not visible).
    4 Create the criteria for the rubric (to add new criteria, click on + Criterion down at the left)
    5 Compose the rubric set
    • If you want to define the grade by these different criteria, then select the option: Use this rubric for assignment grading.
    • If you'd rather only work with comments (in purpose of feedback), then select I'll write free-form comments when assessing students.
    6 Click on Create rubric.

    For more comprehensive information:

    • How do I add a rubric to an assignment?
    • Creating and using Rubrics

    During assessment, the rubric section may seem a bit narrow within your screen. You can make it wider by dragging the bar next to the rubric to the left.

Step 4                You’re nearly there!

                              Students with extra time

  •  

    If this concerns more than 15 students, then please contact your local ICTO team.

    You can give individual students extra time by doing the following:

    1 Go to the menu Assignments.
    2 Click on the name of the assignment.
    3 Click on edit in the upper right.
    4 Scroll all the way down
    5 In the last window, Assign, click the + Add button.
    6 A new window appears
      Write down the name of the student(s) with extra time at the top. Click on the name so that it will be added in this new window.
    7 Enter the new deadline and end date for these students (so including the extra time).
    8 Click on save.
     

                              Publish the assigment

  •  

    Make a final check and get the assignment ready!

    1 Go to the menu Assignments.
    2 Click on the assignment.
    3 Check if all settings are correct by going through them one by one.
      Tip: Pay particular attention to the due date and availability of the test. Are both time restrictions set to the correct time and date?
    4 If all details are correct, then you can publish the Assignment.
     

    To Publish, click on the button Publish at the top of your screen:

    5 The assignment should now be "live". But don't worry! Students can only see the assignment once it is available on the correct time and date.
     

Advice and tips!

  • Always set a due date: students who hand in their assignment after the due date will be marked late.

  • Set a later end time for the 'Available until' option within the edit settingsL about 5 to 10 minutes after the due date. This prevents students with internet problems from being unable to complete the assignment (to submit the document). After the due date has passed, students will still be marked as 'too late' in Canvas.

  • Set a separate due date and until date for students who are entitled to extra time.

What kind of support should I provide during a test?

Support during the test

With support we mean the "live" support for students during the exam. Whom can the students contact for questions about content or technical questions during the test? What form of communication will there be used?

The degree of support depends on the type of exam. For example, less intensive support will be needed for a take home exam as opposed to a multiple-choice test in Canvas.

 

Video on Zoom or communication by mail

For example, you can decide to start a Zoom meeting together with a team of teachers and provide the entry code for this meeting at the exam. Students can then click on the code during the test to ask questions via Zoom. Are there more students in the Zoom waiting room? Then one of the teachers can also create a break out room within one click to discuss the question in person with the student.

You can also choose to answer questions via the Canvas Inbox or by email. But in that case, try to clearly mention to which email-adres students are able to email.

 

Technical problems during the test

" ... have you tried turning it off and on again? "

Fortunately, some start-up problems can be solved quickly. Therefore, let the student first try out the following options:

  • Try using another (or an updated) web browser, such as Firefox (recommended) or Google Chrome (Which browsers does Canvas Support?)
  • Clear your browser and cache history.
  • Check whether a pop-up blocker prevents the website from displaying information. Disable it if possible.
  • Open the link to the exam in an incognito window and log in again.

How do I view and grade the results?

Assessing the results

Actually, nothing has changed about evaluating results in Canvas. If you have little or no experience with conducting assignments or multi-choice quizzes in Canvas. We have listed a number of tips for you:

  1. Hide feedback / grades

    Make sure that grades and any feedback are hidden. It is best to do this before the test has started. But don't worry, fortunately this can still be done after the test has finished. Follow these steps to make sure that students cannot yet see the assessment or grades.

  2. Make agreements about the grading schedule 

    Discuss with the team of teachers who will assess what. For example, agree to divide the assessment by tutorial groups, students by last name, or opt for a horizontal division in which each tutor examines a particular question.

 

Make sure to agree on a division based assessment between colleagues. If teachers are grading on the exact same moment, they will not be able to see each others feedback and/or grade right away. Make sure to be prepared for this! Only when you leave the SpeedGrader (environment in which the assessment for open questions and the take home exam is done), then you are able to view the feedback of colleagues thereafter. It would be very unfortunate to erase any feedback or grades of your colleague!

Inspection session

Feedback / Inspection Session

Since every student has the right to inspection, it is important to facilitate an online inspection session (in some situations the Examinations Board can decide that inspection is not necessary for multiple-choice questions).  This process is different for exams with closed and open questions.

If you want to estimate how many students would like an inspection session, you can set up a survey using Canvas Quizzes. For more information, see: How do I create a survey in my course?

 

Closed questions

 

Open questions / Take home test

How do I provide feedback for closed questions?

You can set a specific date / timeslot in which students can view their answers. You have to set this up after the exam.

  1. Go to the exam settings and check the box for 'Let Students See Their Quiz Responses (Incorrect Questions Will Be Marked In Student Feedback)'
  2. If you want to let students see the correct answers (optional, steps 2-4), check 'Let Students See The Correct Answers'.
  3. Fill in the start date at 'Show Correct Answer at' .
  4. Fill in the end date at 'Hide Correct Answers at'.

The students' responses will be available from the moment you check this box. The quiz answers will only be available between the specified dates. If you do not want to hide the grades at a certain point, you can leave this field blank.

Important!

  • Be aware that if you set this up before the exam, students will be able to see their quiz responses right after they hand in their answers. If other students are still taking their exam, this could be a fraud risk. 
  • Even if you hide the correct answers at a certain point, students can still see their question responses and take screenshots. Take this into consideration when reusing questions on later examinations.

How do I let students inspect open questions / a take-home test?

To let students view their feedback on open questions or the take-home exam, you have to post the grades. By posting the grades, all feedback, comments, and grades given in the Speedgrader will be visible to students. Including the rubrics, if these were used.

  1. Go to 'Grades' and find the column(s) with the examination(s).
  2. Click on the three dots on the right side of the column.
  3. Select 'Post grades'.

More information about posting grades can be found here.
