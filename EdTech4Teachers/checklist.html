  Request web lectures

   NEW! The UvA switched from Mediasite to Panopto

From Mediasite to Panopto
After the Christmas holidays, the web lecture service made a transition to a new supplier, resulting in the replacement of 35 recorders in the lecture halls. Starting from January 8th, lectures will no longer be recorded using Mediasite; instead, they will be recorded using the new system Panopto. The procedure for requesting web lectures will stay unchanged, and utilizing the new system is fairly straightforward.

Accessing old videos
In the meantime, new content can be accessed through Panopto, while old videos can still be viewed on Mediasite, and they will remain accessible until the migration is fully completed.

Technical issues
Technical issues arose during the first week, but they have since been resolved. Unfortunately, there are also some limitations with Panopto, as it is currently not available through the rich content editor in Canvas, unlike Mediasite. This means that teachers can't easily access lectures and embed them as they could before. The web lecture service is working to address this issue, but it is uncertain when this option will be reinstated in the future.
Moreover, in the lecture halls, the message "recording" is currently not displayed on the touch panel as soon as a recording starts. Upon successful recording, the message "not recording" remains on screen. We are working hard to find a solution. Recording lectures still works the same as with Mediasite. Always contact the web lecture service when in doubt about a started recording (020-5252800).

   Request web lectures in the planner

Teachers can request one or several recordings with http://webcollegeplanner.uva.nl/. If you have any questions about web lectures, please contact webcolleges@uva.nl (T: 020 525 2800).

What functionalities can I request with the webcollegeplanner?
Recording          →  A full video recording, visual and audio.
Livestream         →  A streaming link and recording.
Sound + Slides   →  Audio recording without video, but the desktop (powerpoint) included.
Forward             →  Allows you to stream to another room.

Please note:
→  If there's a change of location or room, the request for the recording (or livestream) must be submitted again via the webcollegeplanner.
→  Do not forget to wear the microphone provided by the webcollegedienst during the lecture, otherwise the weblecture recording will contain no audio and/or students online won't hear you.
→  The livestream function has a delay of about 30 seconds which makes it not ideal for communicating with students at home. Alternitavely, a BRIO device is attached in every lecture hall by default and this will allow you to achieve the same goal, only without delay. Email icto-csw@uva.nl if you have any questions about it.

The UvA participates in the national Easy Access buyout scheme (2023-2025). This allows you to use or reuse copyrighted material for educational purposes. This applies when you – for example – include magazine articles, book chapters or images in a reader, a presentation, Canvas or Teams.

   Establish which basic rules you must follow for linking sources

For textual sources, the following conditions apply:
→ Maximum of 40 pages from a publication;
→ No more than 20% of the original publication.

For example:
Does a publication count 100 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of 20 pages from this. Does a publication count 20 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of four pages.

Please note that 'original publication' means the number / issue / edition of the journal in which the article was published.

→ Here, you can read on what to do if you do not meet these conditions

   Brush up your knowledge on which things are always allowed

In terms of linking sources, there are many cases where the above mentioned conditions do not apply. Below it is described which cases are always allowed, but you can also always use the Copyright Checker:

→ Linking to materials in the Library Catalogue CataloguePlus;
→ Linking to Open Access publications (with or without a Creative Commons licence);
→ Linking to any other place where a publication can legally be accessed;
→ Uploading material from databases for which the Library has a licence, if the database allows this.
→ Uploading non-commercial educational material created by yourself or at the UvA;
→ Referring to materials and having students look up the physical publication themselves;
→ Using or reusing publications that are in the public domain;
→ Linking to or embedding audiovisual material. You may show audiovisual material to aid in teaching on-site. Uploading audiovisual material in a digital learning environment is not allowed.

  Set up your Canvas course

   Upload the study guide

It is important to share a study guide (course syllabus) with your students, so they are aware of the course program and schedule. We advise you to use Canvas for this purpose. It is helpful to place the study guide in a prominent place, somewhere not to be missed. Teachers commonly use one of the two options below:

1) Upload the study guide to your course home page
First, create a new page, add some text and upload your study guide onto the page. Then, set your new page as the course home page.

→ How do I create a new page in a course?
→ How do I upload a PDF to a page in a course?
→ How do I change the Course Home Page?

2) Upload the study guide to Modules
Go to Modules in your course menu and create a new module by clicking on the upper right button +Module. Now, add the study guide to this module. You can do so by clicking on the upper right button + within the module. Then you can select the guide as a file from your local computer.

→ How do I add course content as module items?

   Organize your Canvas course

Use the following tips to organize your Canvas course and keep it neat and tidy. This not only benefits your students, but also yourself and your colleagues.

1) Use the course menu Modules as your course home page
You can make use of many options and features in Canvas, such as a Pages, an Assignment or a File. For each one of these, there's a different menu item. However, there is one exception: Modules. If you're using modules, you can actually import and combine all these elements together to create a clear overview. Instead of students having to click through different kinds of course menus and items, you can now provide a structured setup.

→ How do I use modules in my Canvas course?
→ How do I change the Course Home Page?

2) Minimize your course menu
For each course, there are at least 20 different kinds of menus to make use of, you are probably not going to use all of these. We strongly recommend that you hide any parts you're not using, otherwise students aren't able to properly navigate the course content.

→ How do I edit the Course Navigation Menu?

   Plan a meeting with ICTO to set up your grade calculation

Did you have difficulty preparing the calculation set-up last year? Or did you run into some issues submitting the grades to SIS? Perhaps you just have some very complicated ideas on how you’d like to calculate the final grade. Whatever the case, we strongly encourage you to set up a meeting with us. Even more so if you have never worked with Final Grades before. A short meeting with the ICTO team will provide you with all the knowledge you need.

We often hear from teachers that these kinds of meetings take away a lot of stress, and they make the process not only easier but also more enjoyable. By doing this way ahead of time, submitting grades to SIS will be a breeze when the moment comes!

Make an (online or offline) appointment with us by selecting 'Final Grades' in the booking form, and we’ll sit down with you to walk you through setting up your Final Grades menu.

  Set up your digital exam(s)

   NEW! Read about our new digital examination software (coming in september)

From TestVision to Ans Exam
From september 2024 onwards the UvA will transition to a new software system for digital exams. This is the result of a statuatory European tender. Two suppliers had registered for this tender. After an extensive evaluation, Ans Exam emerged as the best choice. The current supplier, TestVision, came in second. This means that all faculties of the UvA will gradually switch to Ans Exam during the upcoming academic year. Click here to read the frequently asked questions about the transition from TestVision to Ans.

Duration of the contract
With Ans Exam, the UvA brings in an innovative partner for the long term, with a contract of at least 6 years, extendable up to a maximum of 15 years. This long duration forms the basis for stable service and collaboration. ANS Exam has extensive experience in higher education with digital examinations. The continuous development and innovation of Ans Exam will also be shaped based on the specific wishes of the UvA.

About the new functionalities
Ans Exam offers fresh and new functionalities. We are excited and believe that teachers will be better able to handle functionalities related to constructing and reviewing an exam. Here is a summary about the new functionalities (#enroll here first).

The ICTO team sets up a copy of last year's exam with copies of last year's questions and test settings – or if this is your first time, we'll set up concept questions and assign basic settings to the exam.

Teachers are solely responsible for modifying the questions (#enroll here to view) in TestVision. Questions can be changed by teachers until the last moment. A request for adding new questions to the test or changing test settings can be sent to ICTO up to one week before the start of the test.

You'll receive automatic emails from ICTO regarding your digital exam. These inform you when an exam is ready for you to work on, and you'll also receive more information about the moment of examination.
