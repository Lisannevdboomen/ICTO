SEPTEMBER '23

Welcome! Thank you for checking out the very first ICTO newsletter of 2023/24! For those of you who don’t know us very well yet: this newsletter is created by the ICTO team. Here we share all kinds of ICT-related news for teachers.

The Social Sciences ICTO-team consists of five members in total, and our job is to support teachers in the use of Canvas, (Final) grades and digital exams, among other things. On this page, you can read more about the team and what else we can help you with. If you have questions about any of these topics, please don’t hesitate to contact us!

You can find us at REC B7.08, and you’re free to walk in. Usually there is someone available at the office from Mondays to Thursdays. Of course, you can also e-mail us at icto-csw@uva.nl. We try our best to respond to your messages as soon as possible. If you’d like to make an appointment with the team, you can do so via the button below.

Ans Exam becomes the new digital testing software

Next academic year, the UvA will switch to a new software system for digital testing called Ans Exam! After an extensive evaluation, Ans Exam came out on top. The current supplier, TestVision, came in second. This means that all UvA faculties will switch to Ans Exam in phases in the coming academic year.

With Ans Exam, the UvA acquires an innovative partner for the long term with a contract of at least 6 years with an extension option to a maximum of 15 years. This long term is the basis for a stable service and cooperation. Our team is very excited to get started. The many functionalities and friendly design promise great things. During the current academic year we will run pilot exams to get more familiar with the new system, that way we will prepare well for the start next year.

For now, you can use our current digital exam instructions on TestVision. The instructions have been completely redesigned and updated!

Persuall info page

Have you checked out our new Perusall information page yet? Perusall enables your students to collaboratively annotate documents of your choice. Its intended use is to stimulate students to complete the required reading before lectures, so that you can continue discussions or address questions during the lecture itself.

Our recently updated information page explains more about how to get started with this tool through a handy roadmap and useful tips & tricks. Another great addition to the page is the 'live example’. This shows you an example of the student view of a Perusall assignment.

Check it out and don't hesitate to contact ICTO if you need more support or would like to brainstorm about the possibilities in the tool.

FeedbackFruits info page

Also make sure to visit our new FeedbackFruits information page! FeedbackFruits offers a suite of tools that facilitate (online) learning activities. Examples of possiblities are the enrichment of audio, video and documents with questions, automated feedback on papers and the organisation of a peer review assignment. All FeedbackFruits tools are fully integrated into Canvas, meaning the grades sync back seamlessly.

Our recently updated information page features an overview of all tools at your disposal and a flowchart to help you pick the tool that suits your needs. Every tool has an instruction on how to get started and a live example, so you can experience the tool as if you were a student.

Take a look and don't hesitate to contact ICTO if you need support or want to brainstorm about the possibilities of integrating FeedbackFruits in your course.

New copyright regulations: check your teaching materials!

When using or reusing copyrighted material in education, lecturers have to deal with laws and regulations surrounding copyright. For the 2023-2025 period, new rules apply with regard to copyright. Though the new agreement has only just been made public, the arrangements are effective from this year onwards. You are, for example, allowed to copy up to 40 pages (instead of 50) from a publication, provided this constitutes a maximum of 20% of that work. With images and audiovisual material, you must always cite the source. This also applies to, for instance, (PowerPoint) presentations, knowledge clips and web lectures. By using this copyright checker, you can be sure you are on the right track!

Counting discussion responses

The ICTO team has developed a new script with Python that allows us to read data from Canvas Discussions to create a handy overview for teachers. Do you use many discussions to track student participation, but still lose track due to the large volume of submissions? Then email icto-csw@uva.nl and we'll run the data so you receive a nice Excel with the amount of (counting) discussion topics and responses per student. We have already been able to make a teacher very happy with this script and hear that it saves a lot of time!

Revamped: My UvA app

The My UvA app has undergone a complete makeover. The iPhone and Android app now sport the same look as student.uva.nl. Students will need to log in less frequently, and they'll receive push notifications about their timetable and grades.

Research among students revealed that the My UvA app was appreciated for its timetable and push notifications. However, the app was no longer being developed, leading to outdated appearance and technology. The student.uva.nl team has completely rebuilt the app. Now, the app functions the same as the website and will be worked on all the time.

Student Well-Being button

Recently, a new button was added to the left menu bar on Canvas: the Student Well-Being button! Most students have probably already seen the button, but do not hesitate to remind students of its existence!


Kaltura MediaSpace Go
