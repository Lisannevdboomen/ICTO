On this page, you can find a concise checklist containing essential tips to help you prepare your course for the second semester. Go through these points and be sure to start the semester without any worries.

This checklist includes what to think about when setting up your Canvas course and how to organize your Final Grades set-up. In addition, you are reminded of important deadlines around requesting web lectures and how this works. You are also asked to review the new copyright rules and apply them to your teaching materials. Finally, we’d like to make you aware of the process around digital exams. By walking through this digital exam-checklist, you make sure you’re aware of deadlines, you’ll know what to do to prepare your exam and what you can do to schedule an online inspection, for example. Of course, we’ll also briefly discuss the transition to Ans Exam next academic year.

At the bottom of the page, you’ll find a board with some upcoming changes in software that we all use at the UvA. This way, you’ll know exactly what changes are happening and when.

CHECKLIST

  Set up your Canvas course

   Upload your study guide

It is important to share a study guide (course syllabus) with your students, so they are aware of the course program and schedule. We advise you to use Canvas for this purpose. It is helpful to place the study guide in a prominent place, somewhere not to be missed. Teachers commonly use one of the two options below:

1) Upload the study guide to your course home page
Firstly, create a new page, add some text and upload your study guide onto the page. Then, set your new page as the course home page.

2) Upload the study guide to Modules
Go to Modules in your course menu and create a new module by clicking on the upper right button +Module. Now, add the study guide to this module. You can do so by clicking on the upper right button + within the module. Then you can select the guide as a file from your local computer.

   Organize your Canvas course

Use the following tips to organize your Canvas course and keep it neat and tidy. This not only benefits your students, but also yourself and your colleagues.

1) Use the course menu Modules as your course home page
You can make use of many options and features in Canvas, such as a Pages, an Assignment or a File. For each one of these, there's a different menu item. However, there is one exception: Modules. If you're using modules, you can actually import and combine all these elements together to create a clear overview. Instead of students having to click through different kinds of course menus and items, you can now provide a structured setup.

2) Minimize your course menu
For each course, there are at least 20 different kinds of menus to make use of, you are probably not going to use all of these. We strongly recommend that you hide any parts you're not using, otherwise students aren't able to properly navigate the course content.

   Publish your course

Every aspect of Canvas works on a 'Publish' and 'Unpublish' basis. Want to put your Canvas course live? Or share a page with students? Publish it! Manage the visibility of specific content by using the publish and unpublish button and use 'Student View' if you want to double check.

Please note: you are expected to have published your Canvas course page no later than two weeks before the start of your course.

   Double-check your course using student view

Student view allows teachers to view a Canvas course as their students would. This is a useful tool to double-check if your course is set up properly (e.g. to check if the aspects of your course that you wish to be published, are actually visible to students).

Make sure to check whether the correct course menu items on the left are visible to students and whether the correct modules are published along with their contents.

   Communicate with your students

After you have set up the basic elements of your course and put it live, you can send students an Announcement. Do you want to approach students individually? In that case, use the Canvas Inbox!

Tip: if your course is not live yet but you would like to contact students anyway, you are advised to briefly publish the course, send the message, and then unpublish the course again after this.

   Manage your gradebook

Students like to have a good overview of their results and obtain feedback on the assignments they submit. At the UvA, students receive their official grades in Student Information System (SIS). In Canvas, you can use the Gradebook or Grades to show the results of the assignments. The Canvas SpeedGrader allows you to grade and give feedback on the documents which students submit for an assignment.

  Organize your Final Grades set-up

   Set up your two-step verification

The UvA highly values working safely online. In pursuit of this, two-step verification is being applied to an increasing number of applications. To make sure you can access all UvA systems when you log in, it is important that you make sure your two-step verification is all set up.

Microsoft Authenticator
To access Microsoft 365, you will need to install and activate the Microsoft Authenticator app on your mobile phone.
→ How do I activate Microsoft Authenticator?

Tiqr
Many UvA information systems that require access via the internet will feature two-step verification using Tiqr. For instance, you’ll need Tiqr to transfer final grades to SIS or to access TestVision.
→ How do I activate my Tiqr?

Are you experiencing difficulties activating your two-step verification? Please contact the ICTS Servicedesk via 020 525 1402.

   Plan a meeting with ICTO to set up your grade calculation

Did you have difficulty preparing the calculation set-up last year? Or did you run into some issues submitting the grades to SIS? Perhaps you just have some very complicated ideas on how you’d like to calculate the final grade. Whatever the case, we strongly encourage you to set up a meeting with us. Even more so if you have never worked with Final Grades before. A short meeting with the ICTO team will provide you with all the knowledge you need.

We often hear from teachers that these kinds of meetings take away a lot of stress, and they make the process not only easier but also more enjoyable. By doing this way ahead of time, submitting grades to SIS will be a breeze when the moment comes!

Make an (online or offline) appointment with us by selecting 'Final Grades' in the booking form, and we’ll sit down with you to walk you through setting up your Final Grades menu.

   Check out ICTO's instructions on how to transfer final grades to SIS

The Final grades menu has been developed by the UvA to help teachers calculate, communicate and share final grades in Canvas. This might not be on the top of your list at the start of your course, but we still encourage you to browse through ICTO’s Final Grades instructions. These describe in detail how to prepare your grade calculation set-up and how to eventually register final grades in SIS.

The isntructions also describe some issues you could encounter using the tool. For example, what do you do when the red Submit grades button is not red? And what does the hourglass icon in front of the students actually mean?

You can find the instructions on how to use Final grades here, but don’t hesitate to reach out if you need advice or have other questions.

  Review the updated copyright regulations

   Read up on the Easy Access agreement 2023 (EAR II) and its conditions

The UvA participates in the national Easy Access buyout scheme (2023-2025). This allows you to use or reuse copyrighted material for educational purposes. This applies when you – for example – include magazine articles, book chapters or images in a reader, a presentation, Canvas or Teams.

→ You can read all about the conditions your teaching materials must meet here

  • Back to top

 

   Establish which basic rules you must follow for linking sources

For textual sources, the following conditions apply:
→ Maximum of 40 pages from a publication;
→ No more than 20% of the original publication.

For example:
Does a publication count 100 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of 20 pages from this. Does a publication count 20 pages? In that case, you may copy a maximum of four pages.

Please note that 'original publication' means the number / issue / edition of the journal in which the article was published.

→ Here, you can read on what to do if you do not meet these conditions

   Brush up your knowledge on which things are always allowed

In terms of linking sources, there are many cases where the above mentioned conditions do not apply. Below it is described which cases are always allowed, but you can also always use the Copyright Checker:

→ Linking to materials in the Library Catalogue CataloguePlus;
→ Linking to Open Access publications (with or without a Creative Commons licence);
→ Linking to any other place where a publication can legally be accessed;
→ Uploading material from databases for which the Library has a licence, if the database allows this.
→ Uploading non-commercial educational material created by yourself or at the UvA;
→ Referring to materials and having students look up the physical publication themselves;
→ Using or reusing publications that are in the public domain;
→ Linking to or embedding audiovisual material. You may show audiovisual material to aid in teaching on-site. Uploading audiovisual material in a digital learning environment is not allowed.
