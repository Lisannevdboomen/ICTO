Final Grades check with ICTO

How do I submit final grades to SIS? Where can I add a resit? In what way could I correct a grade? Are the grades visible to my students? Help!

Above is a selection of the questions we receive most frequently from teachers. Often, teachers only realize things are not going quite as planned right at the moment final grades need to be transferred to SIS. We understand that this might be stressful. After all, your students are waiting for their grades!

This academic year, the ICTO team would like to take away a bit of this stress by simply getting ahead of that moment. Make an appointment with us by selecting 'Final Grades support' in the booking form, and we’ll sit down with you to walk you through setting up your Final Grades menu.

Did you have difficulty preparing the calculation set-up last year? Or did you run into some issues submitting the grades to SIS? Perhaps you just have some very complicated ideas on how you’d like to calculate the final grade. Whatever the case, we strongly encourage you to set up a meeting with us. Even more so if you have never worked with Final Grades before. A short meeting with the ICTO team will provide you with all the knowledge you need.

We often hear from teachers that these kinds of meetings take away a lot of stress, and they make the process not only easier but also more enjoyable. By doing this way ahead of time, submitting grades to SIS will be a breeze when the moment comes! You can make an appointment via the link below! This can be done online or on location.

 New & improved: TestVision
  • TestVision
  • digital exams
  • new release

At the end of September, TestVision released a new and improved version in which quite a few changes were made. In this section, we’ll share the updates we consider most relevant to you as a teacher. Starting with a very much long-awaited update: it is now possible to adjust the answer model for open questions after the exam has been made by your students! In other words, you will now be able to finetune your answer model, for example, while grading your students. To the great frustration of many teachers, this was not possible earlier.

Importantly, it is only possible for you to change the answer model if you have the proper rights in TestVision. Additionally, you will only be able to do so if results have not yet been validated, which fits in nicely with the next update: teachers can now reverse the validation of results in TestVision (if they have been assigned these rights, of course).

Another thing we’d like to give you an update on is the trash can in TestVision! This option has not been released recently, but it is something we just discovered. So: be sure to let us know if you have accidentally deleted a question, as we can easily retrieve it for you!

If you’ve got any questions about these updates, please contact us via email. We’d be more than happy to help. We strongly recommend you read our new and improved TestVision page, as well.

From November 2022 onwards, the UvA switches from the plagiarism detection software Ouriginal to Turnitin Similarity. From that moment, plagiarism checks in Canvas are performed using Turnitin Similarity. This transition takes place because the agreement between the UvA and Ouriginal expired on the 1st of October. Additionally, the UvA found that the product did not meet their expectations. Therefore, the decision was made to look for a more suitable option. Based on wishes and requirements, the plagiarism software Turnitin Similarity has been chosen.

In the spotlight...

In each newsletter we'll highlight one tool and explain what the tool can be used for and why exactly it is so useful. This time, we'll talk about the external tool FeedbackFruits! This one offers various features that allow students to have a more engaging and active interaction with each other, with their teacher, and with the study material. Since there’s so much you can do with this tool, we’ve decided to highlight only three features. Interested in the others? Find out more here.

Do you use YouTube videos a lot? → Interactive Video

What is it?
Increase student engagement by adding in-line questions or discussion threads to specific videos and also decide which ones are compulsory to answer before proceeding to the next. By using this feature, students can watch informative videos, answer your questions, and contribute to the discussion threads.

Why use it?
A great benefit of (online) teaching is the use of multimedia materials to increase engagement, especially video recordings. However, students are often passively consuming the recorded material assigned outside of class, thus coming to lectures unprepared, and teachers have to explain the content again. By adding questions or discussion prompts to these videos students’ engagement increases, and it ensures a deeper understanding of the course content. A great advantage for you as a teacher is that this feature is very accessible and easy to use, as it only requires you to add the questions or discussion prompts.

Do you want more interaction during your lectures? → Interactive Presentation

What is it?
In an interactive presentation you can upload your PowerPoint presentation and add questions or attachments. These can be multiple-choice or open questions, and you can choose at which points in the presentation you’d like to ask them. This way you can track students’ performance and participation in real-time as they answer, and you can gain insights into their understanding of certain concepts. The great thing is, you can do all this without ever switching tabs!

Why use it?
This tool is meant to make presentations more engaging, by encouraging attentiveness and active thinking, as well as helping you to keep track of your students’ progress and level of understanding. It’s one of the few features you can actually use during lectures. However, it is also still possible for students to join in from home. The tool makes it easier for you to see which students actively participate and attend the lecture. Moreover, you’ll have better insights into students’ knowledge, which makes it easier for you to make appropriate interventions or adjustments to the lecture you're giving. One of the advantages of this tool is the option for you to export the data afterwards.

Do your students often work together? → Peer Review

What is it?
In FeedbackFruits’ Peer Review, students are required to hand in an assignment, provide feedback on their peers’ work using predefined rubrics or criteria, and they will be able to reflect on the received comments to improve their final product. As a teacher, you will then be able to track their performance to make appropriate interventions or adjustments to your curriculum.

Why use it?
Even though this feature is not as accessible as others that are provided by FeedbackFruits, it is one of the more popular ones. This is mainly due to the large number of options that exist within the tool. For instance, you can ask students to submit assignments or provide feedback individually or in groups. Additionally, you can create predefined rubrics or criteria which can be used for peer feedback. Besides using these criteria to give feedback to others' work, students can also use them to assess their own work. This helps you to compare how students reflect on their own performance versus that of others. Moreover, feedback can be seen by students instantly, but you can also choose to do so after a certain deadline to avoid bias. Finally, one of the more popular options available is the option to submit and give feedback anonymously.

Any questions about these or other FeedbackFruits features? Shoot us a message via email.

Yes, there’s a way to undelete content in Canvas! It sometimes happens that pages, assignments, quizzes, discussions or announcements are accidentally deleted, and we can imagine that this causes some panic. However, there’s no need to worry, as it is now possible to restore deleted content.

Canvas Confusions

In the past, teachers have told us many times Canvas can be confusing. Usually this is due to the terms that are used. Therefore, we have listed the most confusing terms and tools in Canvas and written down how they can be used most effectively below. Have you run into other confusing terms which aren’t listed below? Feel free to email us!

Section vs. Group  

In Canvas, students can be grouped in Sections or Groups. Curious where to find both? Click here.

  • Go to the People page of your Canvas course
  • Groups (1) can be found in the tabs on the right of the tab Everyone
  • Sections (2) can be found in the column 'Section' in the tab Everyone

Sections in Canvas are used to segment the people in a class: they are linked to separate work groups in SIS. When students register for a course, they’re actually registering for a section. Think about Sections as enabling more streamlined student administration. Use Sections to...

  • Manage grading when there are multiple teachers
  • Assign different due dates and/or assignments to different sections
  • Filter the gradebook by section

Groups provide students with a collaborative environment where they can work together on group projects and assignments. Think about Groups as enabling teamwork. Use Groups to...

  • Create a work space for each group where files, pages and discussions can be shared
  • Create group assignments where only one group member has to submit something for their group
  • Assign grades automatically to each group member (but it is possible to override that setting)

Grades vs. Final Grades

In Canvas, there are two grading menus: Grades (which is also known as ‘the Gradebook’) and Final Grades.

Grades enables instructors to easily view and enter partial grades for students. Depending on the Grade display type, grades for each assignment can be viewed as points, percentages, complete or incomplete, GPA scale, or letter grades. Only graded assignments, graded discussions, graded quizzes, and graded surveys that have been published display in the Gradebook. FAQ’s about the Gradebook can be found on the following page.

Final Grades offers teachers a fast and easy way to submit grades from Canvas to SIS using Two-Factor Authentication. It also allows you to calculate final grades from different components: set up your grade calculation by using partial grades from the Gradebook, excel imports, etc. Click here to find out how you can design the grade calculation set-up in Final Grades. After you have set up the grade calculation, it is possible to publish the final grades and grade calculation to students before transferring them to SIS.

Announcements vs. Inbox

There are multiple ways of communicating with your students. Announcements and Inbox are two of them.

Announcements can be used to send messages or updates to all students or to certain Sections (not to specific Groups!). They show up on the Announcements page of your Canvas course and students receive a notification in their personal mailbox. Several advantages of using Announcements are listed below:

  • Easily link within Canvas (a due date reminder, for example, could link straight to the assignment)
  • Pin announcements to the top of the course’s homepage so it is the first thing students see
  • Access past Announcements and re-use them in future courses
  • Add different media such as pictures and videos
  • Delay posting announcements and edit the announcement up until the post date and time

Inbox is the email feature in Canvas. Students can email all teachers or a specific teacher, and teachers can email all students in a course, students in a specific section, individual students or a group of students. It offers secure communication within Canvas. It is advised to use Inbox for one-on-one communication with students. By default, students and teachers will receive notifications in their personal mailbox whenever they receive a message in their Canvas mailbox. Importantly, beware to respond to messages in Canvas instead of responding to the email notification!

In the spotlight...

In each newsletter we'll highlight one tool and explain what the tool can be used for and why exactly it is so useful. This time, we'll talk about using rubrics to grade assignments in Canvas. Below you can read more about all the things you can do with this tool. Curious as to why you should use rubrics in the first place? Click here.

Why use rubrics?
Have your students ever asked why you graded them in a certain way or commented on the fact that they never knew that they were being graded on a certain component?

As a grading tool, rubrics can address these and other issues related to assessment: they reduce grading time; they increase objectivity and reduce subjectivity; they convey timely, effective feedback to students and they improve students’ ability to understand expectations and include required elements of an assignment. Additionally, rubrics can also facilitate peer-review and self-assessment by setting evaluation standards.

Rubrics

A rubric is an assessment tool for communicating expectations of quality. They are typically comprised of rows and columns. Rows are used to define the various criteria being used to assess an assignment, and columns are used to define levels of performance for each criterion. In Canvas, teachers can design their own rubrics and use them for grading purposes. Below are the many possible options when using rubrics in Canvas:

  • Use rubrics for (multiple) assignments, graded discussions and quizzes.
    • How do I add a rubric to an assignment?
    • How do I add a rubric to a graded discussion?
    • How do I add a rubric to a quiz?
  • Re-use rubrics you have designed as a teacher for another course.
  • Enable free-form comments instead of using a ratings scale for each criteria. If this is done, there is no exact scale which tells you how many points should be assigned. Teachers can choose the number of points.
  • Remove points from the rubric. No points are then associated with the rubric, but students can still be rated using the criterion.
  • Add comments to each criterion. This option is available regardless of whether you choose to assign points or not.
  • Use rubrics for automatic grading. If you set your rubric for grading, the rubric points will automatically calculate a grade in the grade field of SpeedGrader. Otherwise, you can enter the grade from the rubric manually. (Note: this option is not available for quizzes).
    • How do I use a rubric to grade submissions in SpeedGrader?
  • Hide the total score of a rubric. Students can still see the point values for each criterion, but the total score will not be shown at the bottom of the rubric. This option is only available if the rubric is not used for grading.
  • Easily convert the number of points obtained in a rubric to a grade from 1 to 10 in the Final Grades calculation set-up.
    • How to convert total points of an exercise to a 10 point scale in the Final Grades tool
